SAP is a major ERP vendor whose GRC suite (SAP Access Control,
SAP Process Control, SAP Risk Management) implements the access
governance controls tested in [[SOX Section 404]] audits.

Key facts:
- SAP Access Control 10.1 provides four core capability areas:
  Access Requests (workflow approvals with risk checks), Access
  Risk Analysis (the [[Segregation of Duties]] engine), Business
  Role Management, and [[Emergency Access Management]]
  ("firefighter" IDs with owners, controllers, reason codes, and
  full session logging).
- Where SoD conflicts cannot be removed, SAP assigns "mitigating
  controls" — its term for [[Compensating Controls]] — to users,
  roles, or profiles with owners, monitors, and validity periods.
- Periodic [[User Access Review]] campaigns, provisioning logs, and
  firefighter [[Audit Trail]] reports provide re-performable
  evidence for [[ICFR]] testing; it also covers non-SAP systems
  such as [[Oracle]] and JD Edwards.
