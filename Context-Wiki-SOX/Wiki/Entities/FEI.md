Financial Executives International (FEI) is a leading organization
of roughly 15,000 CFOs, controllers, treasurers, and other senior
financial executives. It is a [[COSO]] sponsoring organization and
speaks for preparers in SOX rulemaking.

Key facts:
- Its February 2007 comment letter to the [[PCAOB]] (Docket 021)
  supported the top-down, risk-based AS 5 proposal (now
  [[AS 2201]]) while pressing for alignment with [[SEC]] management
  guidance and for changed inspection practices; the final standard
  adopted much of what FEI advocated.
- FEI's Committee on Corporate Reporting (CCR) publishes the
  "ICFR: Insights, Issues and Practices" series, including the
  December 2019 preparer guide on internal control over
  [[Business Combinations]] and [[Purchase Price Allocation]].
- FEI argued that [[IT General Controls]] were over-tested because
  downstream [[Compensating Controls]] usually mitigate ITGC risk.
