The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is the federal
securities regulator. Enforcement authority for the
[[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] rests with the SEC; violations are treated
like violations of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The SEC
also oversees the [[PCAOB]].

Key facts:
- Release 33-8238 (2003) implemented [[SOX Section 404]]: annual
  management report on [[ICFR]], auditor attestation, and filing of
  [[SOX Section 302]] / [[SOX Section 906]] certifications as exhibits.
- Release 33-8810 (2007) gave management voluntary, principles-based
  guidance for a [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] evaluation of ICFR.
- Release 33-8809 (2007) codified the [[Material Weakness]]
  definition and required a single auditor opinion directly on ICFR.
- Published a plain-language Section 404 guide for small business
  and staffed a help channel (404smallbusiness@sec.gov).
- Newer SEC cybersecurity and climate disclosure rules add burden on
  [[Disclosure Controls and Procedures]] design.
