KPMG is one of the Big Four audit and advisory firms. Its SOX
publications span the first compliance wave through modern program
maturity assessment.

Key facts:
- The 2004 "SOX Section 404 Management's Assessment Process" FAQ
  walks management through planning/scoping, documenting and
  testing design and operating effectiveness, correcting
  deficiencies, and reporting — including illustrative minimum
  sample sizes (annual control 1; daily 15-30; recurring manual
  30-60) for the [[Management Assessment Process]].
- The SOX Health Check (2016 brief) rates [[ICFR]] program maturity
  across seven pillars (strategy, risk assessment,
  [[Entity-Level Controls]], control selection, testing strategy,
  evaluating results, governance) using a proprietary continuum of
  nearly 150 data points.
- Its Controls Portfolio Analysis (CPAM) quantifies the
  [[Total Cost of Control]] and supports
  [[Controls Rationalization]].
