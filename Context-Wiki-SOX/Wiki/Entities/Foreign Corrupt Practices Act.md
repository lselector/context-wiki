The Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) of 1977 is the U.S.
federal law whose internal accounting control provisions began the
statutory lineage of [[Internal Control]] over financial reporting.

Key facts:
- SEC Release 33-8238 traces the lineage of internal control from
  the FCPA of 1977 through the Treadway Commission to [[COSO]]'s
  1992 Internal Control — Integrated Framework, the basis of the
  modern [[ICFR]] definition.
- The [[Reasonable Assurance]] standard used in [[SOX Section 404]]
  evaluations is keyed to FCPA language: the level of assurance
  "as would satisfy prudent officials in the conduct of their own
  affairs."
