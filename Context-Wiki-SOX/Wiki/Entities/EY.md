EY (Ernst & Young) is one of the Big Four audit and advisory
firms. Its global SOX survey (published 2020) of publicly traded
companies examines how [[SOX Compliance Program]]s are managed and
enhanced.

Key facts:
- 63% of respondents said SOX effort has increased since initial
  implementation; 87%+ aim for a lean, effective
  [[Internal Control]] framework with heavy [[External Auditor]]
  reliance on management's work.
- [[IT General Controls]] produced the most open deficiencies —
  more [[Significant Deficiency]] and [[Material Weakness]]
  instances than any other aspect of SOX.
- 79% assess third-party service organizations via a
  [[SOC 1 Report]]; 30% of reported material weaknesses were
  attributed to inadequate manual review controls.
- Recommends three themes: connected governance and oversight; a
  responsive, risk-based operating model enabled by technology;
  and continuous program improvement.
