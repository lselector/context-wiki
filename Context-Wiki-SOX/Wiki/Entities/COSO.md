COSO — the Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway
Commission — is the private-sector body whose frameworks are the
leading criteria for designing and assessing [[Internal Control]].
Its sponsoring organizations include the [[AICPA]], [[FEI]], and
[[IIA]].

Key facts:
- The [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] (1992; updated May 2013
  with 5 components and 17 principles) is the framework most US
  public companies use for the [[SOX Section 404]] [[ICFR]]
  assessment; the [[SEC]] cites it as a suitable framework.
- The [[COSO ERM Framework]] (2004; updated 2017, 5 components and
  20 principles) covers [[Enterprise Risk Management]] and is
  complementary to — not a replacement for — the IC framework.
- Its 2009 Guidance on Monitoring Internal Control Systems (authored
  by [[Grant Thornton]]) elaborates
  [[Monitoring of Internal Control]], [[Key Controls]], and
  [[Persuasive Information]].
- A 1994 addendum addressed [[Safeguarding of Assets]]; 2006
  guidance targeted smaller public companies.
