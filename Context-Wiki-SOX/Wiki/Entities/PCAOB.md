The Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) was
established by Title I (Secs. 101-109) of the
[[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] to register, inspect, investigate, and
discipline registered public accounting firms and to set auditing
standards for audits of public companies (issuers).

Key facts:
- Under [[SOX Section 404]](b), the [[External Auditor]] attests to
  ICFR under PCAOB attestation standards — today [[AS 2201]].
- Adopted Auditing Standard No. 5 in Release 2007-005A (June 2007),
  replacing the costly AS 2 with a scalable, top-down, risk-based
  standard, later recodified as [[AS 2201]].
- Coordinated AS 5 with the [[SEC]]'s management guidance (33-8810)
  so both use the same [[Material Weakness]] and
  [[Significant Deficiency]] definitions.
- PCAOB inspections drive audit behavior: inspection reports citing
  insufficient evidence over key reports,
  [[Information Produced by the Entity]], and
  [[Management Review Controls]] push auditors — and management —
  toward more documentation and rising SOX costs.
