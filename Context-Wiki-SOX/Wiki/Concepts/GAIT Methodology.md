GAIT (Guide to the Assessment of IT Risk) is an [[IIA]] principles-based methodology for scoping and evaluating IT risk in [[SOX Section 404]] assessments. The IIA's March 2008 practice guide "GAIT for IT General Control Deficiency Assessment" extends it to deciding whether an [[IT General Controls]] deficiency is a mere control deficiency, a [[Significant Deficiency]], or a [[Material Weakness]], aligned with the post-AS 5 top-down, risk-based approach of the [[SEC]] and [[PCAOB]].

Key facts:

- Core insight: ITGC deficiencies do not directly misstate financial statements — their effect travels through a **"reliance chain"** (key ITGCs → ITGC control objectives → critical IT functionality such as [[IT Application Controls]] and key reports → prevention/detection of material misstatement), i.e., [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] scoping logic in reverse.
- **Six assessment principles**, including the two-part material weakness test (likelihood and impact), staged assessment, and grouping/aggregation of deficiencies by control objective, functionality, and significant account.
- A **10-step assessment process**: from confirming a real control failure through identifying affected control objectives, applications, and critical functionality, to a final "reasonable person" review with aggregation.
- Burden of proof is on demonstrating why a key ITGC failure is NOT a [[Material Weakness]]; keeps findings (e.g., weak [[Change Management]] or [[Access Controls]]) proportionate rather than automatically escalated.
- Considers [[Compensating Controls]] and [[Segregation of Duties]] in mitigating ITGC failures.
