Persuasive information is a core concept of COSO's Guidance on Monitoring Internal Control Systems: monitoring must be based on information that is **sufficient** and **suitable** (relevant, reliable, and timely) to support conclusions about whether [[Internal Control]] is operating effectively. Identifying persuasive information is step 3 of COSO's four-step monitoring design progression (see [[Monitoring of Internal Control]]).

Key facts:

- **Direct information** (observation, reperformance) provides an unobstructed view of control operation and is the most persuasive.
- **Indirect information** (operating statistics, key risk and performance indicators, comparative industry metrics) can flag anomalies but **loses persuasive power over time** and must periodically be revalidated with direct information.
- COSO's summary principles state indirect information can be relied on only for a finite period without direct support.
- COSO Volume III illustrates using operating statistics, key risk indicators, and comparisons with expectations as indirect evidence in practice.
- Monitoring tuned toward more direct information and more formal documentation better supports [[External Auditor]] reliance under [[AS 2201]].
