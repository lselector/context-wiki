A walkthrough traces a single transaction from origination through
the company's processes into the financial records, combining
inquiry, observation, inspection of documents, and re-performance.

Key facts:
- Under [[AS 2201]], walkthroughs are usually the most effective
  way to understand likely sources of misstatement ("what could go
  wrong") and transaction flows, and must be performed by the
  auditor or under direct supervision; they ordinarily suffice to
  test control design effectiveness.
- AS 5 replaced AS 2's rigid walkthrough mandate with
  objectives-based requirements — the walkthrough is the usual
  means, not a mechanical rule ([[FEI]] argued a walkthrough alone
  can suffice for lower-risk and entity-level controls).
- For management, process walkthroughs are the most effective
  design-effectiveness test; inquiry alone is never sufficient.
- EY found only 46% of companies perform true end-to-end
  walkthroughs of a single transaction annually; AI use cases now
  include generating walkthrough documentation from recorded
  meetings.
