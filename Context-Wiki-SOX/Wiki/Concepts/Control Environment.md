The control environment is the first component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]: the set of standards, processes, and structures that provide the basis for carrying out [[Internal Control]] across the organization, with the board and senior management setting the [[Tone at the Top]].

Key facts:

- Covers COSO principles 1-5: commitment to integrity and ethical values; board independence and oversight (relevant to the [[Audit Committee]]); structures, reporting lines, authorities, and responsibilities; commitment to competence; and accountability for internal control responsibilities.
- Under [[AS 2201]], the auditor **must** evaluate the control environment as part of assessing [[Entity-Level Controls]]; control-environment controls are indirect but influence all other controls.
- SEC guidance (Release 33-8810) treats control-environment entity-level controls as central to the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]].
- [[FEI]] guidance on [[Business Combinations]] stresses integrating an acquired target into the acquirer's control environment.
- Parallels the Governance and Culture component of the [[COSO ERM Framework]].
