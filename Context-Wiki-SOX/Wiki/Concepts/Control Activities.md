Control activities are the third component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]: actions established through policies and procedures that help ensure management's directives to mitigate risks are carried out.

Key facts:

- Covers COSO principles 10-12: selecting and developing control activities that mitigate risks; selecting general control activities over technology (see [[IT General Controls]]); and deploying control activities through policies and procedures.
- May be **preventive or detective, manual or automated** — e.g., authorizations, verifications, reconciliations, and business performance reviews.
- [[Segregation of Duties]] is typically built into control activities; where not practical, alternative (compensating) controls are used.
- [[FEI]] guidance illustrates tailoring example control activities to acquisition risks in [[Business Combinations]] — including precision levels, expectations versus actuals, and follow-up on outliers, mapped to financial statement assertions.
- [[EY]] reports an average in-scope key-control mix of ~30% manual controls, plus ITGCs (18%), IT-dependent manual (14%), fully automated application controls (8%), and others.
