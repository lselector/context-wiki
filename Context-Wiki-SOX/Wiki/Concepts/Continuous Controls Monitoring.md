Continuous Controls Monitoring (CCM) is the use of automated tools to monitor controls, transactions, configurations, and access on an always-on basis, replacing periodic manual reviews. COSO's monitoring guidance described early forms (threshold checks, duplicate-payment detection, [[Segregation of Duties]] analysis) as technology enhancing [[Ongoing Monitoring]]; its reliability depends on effective [[IT General Controls]].

Key facts:

- **[[Oracle]]:** Advanced Access Controls continuously monitors access policies and SoD conflicts in ERP Cloud; Fusion Risk Management uses embedded AI and 300+ prebuilt algorithms to monitor millions of transactions, configuration changes, and access events.
- **[[SAP]]:** Access Control's alerting and scheduled background risk-analysis jobs provide CCM over ERP access.
- **[[NetSuite]]:** SuiteAnalytics saved searches and dashboards alert on exceptions such as SoD violations and unusually large transactions.
- **Adoption is still low:** [[EY]] found CCM used often by only 17% of SOX programs; [[Protiviti]] reports advanced analytics users move toward CCM and exception testing (e.g., analyzing 100% of journal entries).
- [[PwC]]'s Level 3 "Value Enhancement" maturity includes CCM tools for critical controls; [[Deloitte]] case studies show second-line controls-automation teams owning CCM.
- CCM reduces audit cost and detects issues between assessment cycles, helping avoid a late-discovered [[Material Weakness]].
