Information Produced by the Entity — commonly abbreviated **IPE** — is the reports and data a company's own systems generate and that are used in performing or testing controls (e.g., system reports feeding a [[Management Review Controls]] review). Because controls relying on IPE are only as good as the underlying data, its **completeness and accuracy** must be validated.

Key facts:

- [[FEI]] guidance requires procedures validating completeness and accuracy of data used in a management review control as a core design question.
- [[EY]]: IPE ranks among the most challenging areas for reliable, sustainable controls (49%, second only to IT general/application controls); IPE controls make up about 10% of the average in-scope key-control mix.
- [[Protiviti]]: [[PCAOB]] inspection reports cite audits with insufficient procedures over key reports and IPE, pushing [[External Auditor]]s — and management — toward more documentation; accuracy/reliability of key reports is a top auditor focus area (25%).
- Key reports and IPE sit in the GAIT "reliance chain": their consistent operation depends on [[IT General Controls]] over the applications that produce them.
