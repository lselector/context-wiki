Published by [[COSO]] in June 2017 (authored by [[PwC]]), Enterprise Risk Management — Integrating with Strategy and Performance updates the 2004 ERM — Integrated Framework. It positions [[Enterprise Risk Management]] as "the culture, capabilities, and practices" integrated with strategy-setting to manage risk in creating, preserving, and realizing value — not a function, department, or checklist. It is complementary to the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]; neither supersedes the other.

Key facts:

- **Five components:** Governance and Culture; Strategy and Objective-Setting; Performance; Review and Revision; and Information, Communication, and Reporting.
- **Twenty principles** across the components — e.g., board risk oversight, defining desired culture, defining [[Risk Appetite]], evaluating alternative strategies, identifying/assessing/prioritizing risk, developing a portfolio view, and reporting on risk, culture, and performance.
- Highlights risk in **strategy selection**: the possibility of strategy misaligning with mission/vision/values, and the risk implications of the strategy chosen.
- Components parallel internal control disciplines: Governance and Culture ↔ [[Control Environment]] and [[Tone at the Top]]; Performance ↔ [[Risk Assessment]]; Review and Revision ↔ [[Monitoring of Internal Control]]; Information, Communication, and Reporting ↔ [[Information and Communication]].
- Benefits: increased range of opportunities, fewer negative surprises, less performance variability, better resource deployment, enhanced resilience; board oversight duties intersect with [[Audit Committee]] responsibilities under the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]].
