A user access review (UAR) — also called **access certification** — is a periodic (typically quarterly or annual) campaign in which managers verify that users' system access and role assignments still match their job duties, revoking what is no longer needed. It is a standard [[IT General Controls]] expectation in [[SOX Section 404]] audits.

Key facts:

- [[NetSuite]] guidance: periodic UARs prevent "role bloat" and confirm assignments match duties; saved searches list effective permissions per user/role for reviewers.
- [[SAP]] Access Control's Periodic Reviews module runs recurring UAR and SoD review campaigns with workflow, reviewer assignment, and history reporting — evidence packages for [[External Auditor]] testing.
- [[Oracle]]: access certification workflows document SoD analysis results and force line-manager review of privileges; the Fusion RMC "Certification Advisor" AI agent generates plain-language briefings (role contents, peer comparison, incident history) for each certification decision, producing documented rationale and stronger audit evidence.
- [[Protiviti]]: gaps in user access reviews and terminations are among the most common sources of IT access deficiencies (the top SOX challenge area at 44%).
- [[Deloitte]] case study: an engineering compliance team under the CIO runs control automation and user access reviews for the SOX program.
