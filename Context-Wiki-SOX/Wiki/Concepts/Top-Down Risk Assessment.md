The top-down risk assessment is the organizing approach of both
the [[SEC]]'s management guidance (33-8810) and the [[PCAOB]]'s
[[AS 2201]]: begin with financial-statement-level risks, focus on
[[Entity-Level Controls]], then work down to significant accounts
and disclosures, relevant assertions, and the controls that
address them.

Key facts:
- Replaced the bottom-up, checklist mentality of AS 2; AS 5 dropped
  requirements to separately identify "major classes of
  transactions" and "significant processes."
- Effort follows risk: no need to test controls that, even if
  deficient, present no reasonable possibility of material
  misstatement; evidence scales with the risk of each control.
- IT scoping is integral, not separate: only
  [[IT General Controls]] needed for the proper operation of
  controls addressing [[Financial Reporting Risks]] are evaluated.
- The [[GAIT Methodology]] reliance chain is this scoping logic
  traveled in reverse when assessing ITGC deficiencies.
- EY recommends refreshing scoping at least semiannually rather
  than once per year (46% scope only at year start).
