Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS) are the auditing
standards for audits of non-issuers (private companies and other
entities outside [[PCAOB]] jurisdiction), issued by the [[AICPA]]
Auditing Standards Board as Statements on Auditing Standards and
codified as the AU-C sections.

Key facts:
- The AU-C sections resulted from the AICPA Clarity Project, which
  aligned GAAS with International Standards on Auditing; each
  section has a uniform structure (Introduction, Objectives,
  Definitions, Requirements, Application material).
- Organized by series: 200s general principles (AU-C 200, 230, 240
  fraud), 300s-400s risk assessment (AU-C 315), 500s evidence,
  600s using others' work (AU-C 600, 610), 700s reporting, 800s
  special considerations, 900s U.S. special considerations.
- For SOX-adjacent work the key section is [[AU-C 940]] on the
  [[Integrated Audit]] of [[ICFR]]; also relevant: AU-C 265
  (communicating internal control matters), AU-C 402 (service
  organizations), AU-C 610 (internal auditors).
- Issuer audits instead follow PCAOB standards such as [[AS 2201]].
