Reasonable assurance is the standard of confidence that
[[Internal Control]] and [[ICFR]] are required to provide. It is
deliberately less than absolute assurance.

Key facts:
- Keyed to [[Foreign Corrupt Practices Act]] language: the level of
  assurance "as would satisfy prudent officials in the conduct of
  their own affairs."
- Not absolute because ICFR can be defeated by human error,
  collusion, or [[Management Override]].
- The "prudent official" test is also the final backstop in
  deficiency severity evaluation under both [[AS 2201]] and the
  [[SEC]]'s management guidance.
- The [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] builds reasonable
  assurance into its very definition of internal control.
