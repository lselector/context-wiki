Total cost of control is the full cost of designing, operating, and testing a company's controls portfolio. [[KPMG]] makes managing it a central question of a healthy [[ICFR]] program — asking "How is the total cost of control managed?" in its SOX Health Check.

Key facts:

- KPMG's Controls Portfolio Analysis (CPAM), combined with the SOX Health Check, provides a comprehensive quantitative view of the controls portfolio, **including the total cost of control and its components**, supporting [[Controls Rationalization]] and process improvements.
- A lower total cost of control is a headline actionable outcome of the Health Check, alongside improved governance, team performance, and program efficiency.
- The concern is echoed across the industry: [[Deloitte]]'s [[SOX Modernization]] targets "reduced total cost of compliance"; [[Protiviti]] found 52% report internal SOX cost increases over two years while budgets tighten.
