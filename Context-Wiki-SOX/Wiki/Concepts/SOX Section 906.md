Section 906 of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] (Title IX, White-Collar
Crime Penalty Enhancements) adds 18 U.S.C. 1350, a criminal
certification requirement that parallels the civil
[[SOX Section 302]] certification.

Key facts:
- Each periodic report containing financial statements must be
  accompanied by a written CEO/CFO statement certifying that the
  report fully complies with Exchange Act Sections 13(a)/15(d) and
  fairly presents, in all material respects, the financial
  condition and results of operations.
- Penalties: knowing violation — up to $1,000,000 fine and/or
  10 years in prison; willful violation — up to $5,000,000 and/or
  20 years.
- Per [[SEC]] Release 33-8238, Section 906 certifications are filed
  as exhibits so investors, the SEC, and the Department of Justice
  can monitor compliance via EDGAR.
