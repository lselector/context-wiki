IT application controls are automated controls embedded within business applications — SEC Release 33-8810 cites examples such as **automated matching and edit checks** — that prevent or detect misstatements in transaction initiation, authorization, recording, processing, and reporting.

Key facts:

- Their consistent operation depends on effective [[IT General Controls]]; in the [[GAIT Methodology]] reliance chain, key automated controls and key reports are the "critical IT functionality" that ITGC objectives protect.
- SEC guidance notes automated application controls may be **more efficient to evaluate than manual controls**; [[AS 2201]] treats automated controls as generally lower risk when relevant ITGCs are effective, and allows benchmarking in subsequent years.
- [[KPMG]]: IT scope includes applications and supporting infrastructure behind key processes, control objectives, and relevant assertions, including feeder systems.
- [[EY]]: fully automated application controls average only ~8% of the in-scope key-control mix, and IT general/application controls are the most challenging area for reliable, sustainable controls (64%); EY challenges whether "manual" classifications are still accurate.
- [[Deloitte]] recommends second-line IT skills to support scoping of key IT systems, automated controls, and key reports.
