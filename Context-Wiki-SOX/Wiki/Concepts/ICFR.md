Internal Control over Financial Reporting (ICFR, sometimes ICOFR)
is the financial-reporting subset of [[Internal Control]] that
[[SOX Section 404]] requires management to assess annually. The
term was adopted and defined by [[SEC]] Release 33-8238 (2003).

Key facts:
- Definition: a process designed by or under the supervision of the
  principal executive and financial officers to provide
  [[Reasonable Assurance]] regarding the reliability of financial
  reporting and preparation of GAAP financial statements.
- Three prongs: (1) records that accurately and fairly reflect
  transactions; (2) transactions recorded per GAAP and
  receipts/expenditures made only per authorization; (3) prevention
  or timely detection of unauthorized acquisition, use, or
  disposition of assets ([[Safeguarding of Assets]]).
- Deliberately narrower than the full
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] (excludes operations and
  broad compliance objectives) and than
  [[Disclosure Controls and Procedures]].
- ICFR cannot be considered effective if one or more
  [[Material Weakness]]es exist; inherent limitations include human
  error, collusion, and [[Management Override]].
