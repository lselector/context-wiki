Journal entry controls govern the preparation, review, approval, and posting of journal entries — especially manual and nonstandard entries. They are central to the [[Period-End Financial Reporting Process]] and to addressing [[Management Override]] and [[Fraud Risk]].

Key facts:

- [[AS 2201]] requires the auditor to address controls over journal entries among the fraud-risk / management-override controls (with significant unusual transactions, related parties, and estimates).
- [[KPMG]]'s 404 guidance: period-end controls over journal entries, consolidating adjustments, and drafting statements are critical and must be tested for both annual and quarterly reporting.
- [[FEI]] devotes a section to controls over manual journal entries recorded for an acquisition in [[Business Combinations]].
- [[Protiviti]]: advanced analytics users analyze **100% of journal entries**, focusing on entries outside delegation of authority or booked on nonworking days — a [[Continuous Controls Monitoring]] technique.
- [[NetSuite]] examples: SuiteFlow journal-entry holds and review thresholds, period locking to prevent backdated GL postings, saved-search alerts on large journal adjustments, and separating GL posting from reconciliation ([[Segregation of Duties]]).
