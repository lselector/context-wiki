Tone at the top is the attitude toward integrity, ethical values, and [[Internal Control]] expressed by the board and senior management. In the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]], it anchors the [[Control Environment]] — the set of standards, processes, and structures that provide the basis for internal control.

Key facts:

- COSO's monitoring guidance makes tone at the top the first element of the **foundation for monitoring**: management's and the board's expressed beliefs about the importance of monitoring drive its effectiveness.
- COSO's summary monitoring principles direct the board to oversee monitoring of tone at the top itself and of [[Management Override]] risk — areas senior management cannot objectively self-monitor — often via [[Internal Audit]].
- [[KPMG]]'s SOX 404 guidance lists tone at the top among the company-level controls ([[Entity-Level Controls]]) that management should test.
- The [[COSO ERM Framework]]'s Governance and Culture component parallels the concept: governance "sets the organization's tone" and oversight responsibilities for ERM.
