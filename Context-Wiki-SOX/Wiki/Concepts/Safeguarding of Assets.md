Safeguarding of assets is the third prong of the [[SEC]]'s
[[ICFR]] definition: policies and procedures providing reasonable
assurance regarding prevention or timely detection of unauthorized
acquisition, use, or disposition of assets that could materially
affect the financial statements.

Key facts:
- Rooted in [[COSO]]'s 1994 addendum to the 1992
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] on safeguarding of assets.
- The concept is about authorization, not the quality of business
  judgment: it targets unauthorized transactions, not bad
  decisions (per the [[KPMG]] SOX 404 FAQ).
- In the 2013 COSO framework, safeguarding sits within the
  operations objectives category, but its financial-reporting
  effects put it in [[SOX Section 404]] scope.
