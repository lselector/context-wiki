Purchase Price Allocation (PPA) is the measurement of assets acquired and liabilities assumed at acquisition-date fair value under ASC 805 — per [[FEI]], one of the most critical and complex steps in accounting for [[Business Combinations]].

Key example controls from FEI's guide (each led by a "relevant competent leader," possibly outside accounting):

- Review target data (historical records, audited financial statements) and ASC 805 guidance to ensure all acquired assets and assumed liabilities — including intangibles apart from goodwill — are identified [completeness, existence].
- Review the appropriateness of the valuation approach (income, market, cost) and method (MPEEM, relief from royalty, distributor method) for each significant item [valuation].
- Review the external specialist's valuation end-to-end — prospective financial information (PFI), data, assumptions, methods — with involvement throughout the engagement [accuracy, completeness].
- Review significant PFI components (revenue, opex, EBITDA, capex, depreciation, net working capital) and run sensitivity analyses on key assumptions (growth rates, attrition, royalty rates, discount rates), comparing against historical and industry data.
- Reasonableness check: the share of goodwill relative to consideration transferred (lower for mature targets, higher for growth targets); risk generally rises with transaction significance.
