Risk appetite is the amount and type of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of value. In the [[COSO ERM Framework]] (2017), establishing a risk appetite aligned with strategy is a core element of the Strategy and Objective-Setting component, and "Defines risk appetite" is Principle 7 of the framework's twenty principles.

Key facts:

- The board and management weigh each alternative strategy's risk profile against the entity's risk appetite when selecting strategy.
- In the Performance component, risks to strategy and business objectives are identified, assessed by severity, and prioritized **in the context of risk appetite**, and a portfolio view of risk is developed.
- Board risk oversight includes reviewing and challenging proposed strategy and risk appetite, significant business decisions, and responses to performance deviations — duties that intersect with [[Audit Committee]] responsibilities in public companies.
- Risk appetite connects [[Enterprise Risk Management]] to performance rather than treating risk as an isolated compliance exercise.
