Management review controls (MRCs) are controls in which management reviews estimates, analyses, or reports — comparing them to expectations and investigating items exceeding defined thresholds. They dominate complex, judgmental areas such as [[Business Combinations]] and accounting estimates.

Key facts:

- [[FEI]] design/operation questions: the control's objective and risks addressed; the owner's competence and authority; validation of completeness and accuracy of data used (see [[Information Produced by the Entity]]); what is reviewed (assumptions, data, methodology); thresholds calibrated to detect a [[Material Weakness]]-level misstatement; and follow-up procedures.
- Recommended evidence: lists of follow-up items and resolutions, retained iterations of calculations, reviewer/preparer email threads, and documented review meetings — what the [[External Auditor]] needs under [[AS 2201]].
- [[EY]]: MRCs are among the most challenging areas for reliable, sustainable controls (41%); **30% of reported material weaknesses** were attributed to inadequate manual review controls or deficiencies in existing review controls; 82% of companies have designed thresholds for review controls.
- [[Protiviti]]: [[PCAOB]] inspection reports cite insufficient audit procedures over MRCs (along with key reports and IPE), pressuring more documentation.
