---
type: concept
title: Key Controls
description: Controls whose failure could materially affect objectives without timely detection by other controls, or whose operation prevents or detects other control failures.
tags: [key-controls, monitoring, scoping]
---

Key controls are the subset of controls that matter most for a
given risk. COSO's monitoring guidance defines them as controls
whose failure might materially affect objectives without being
detected timely by other controls, or whose operation prevents or
detects failures of other controls.

Key facts:
- Identifying key controls is step 2 of COSO's monitoring design
  progression (after prioritizing risks, before identifying
  [[Persuasive Information]]); key controls may reside in any of
  the five components, including the [[Control Environment]].
- KPMG's FAQ advises prioritizing controls ("key/primary" or
  high/medium/low) and picking the control point in the process
  flow that best addresses each risk — redundant controls need not
  all be tested.
- EY's survey found ~30% of in-scope key controls are classified as
  manual, alongside ITGCs (18%), IT-dependent manual (14%), and
  fully automated controls (8%).
- Deloitte's precision-testing message: sharper focus on risks and
  key controls moves programs away from a checklist approach and
  reduces cost.
