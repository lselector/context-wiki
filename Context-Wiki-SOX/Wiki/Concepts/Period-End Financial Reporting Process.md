The period-end financial reporting process is the close process:
procedures to record journal entries and consolidating
adjustments, and to draft the financial statements and
disclosures. It is treated as a critical [[Entity-Level Controls]]
area.

Key facts:
- [[AS 2201]] requires the auditor to evaluate the period-end
  financial reporting process (along with the
  [[Control Environment]]) in every [[Integrated Audit]];
  [[AU-C 940]] carries the same requirement for non-issuers.
- KPMG's FAQ calls controls over journal entries, consolidating
  adjustments, and drafting of statements and disclosures critical,
  and says they must be tested for both annual and quarterly
  reporting.
- Manual journal entries in this process are a classic
  [[Fraud Risk]] / [[Management Override]] vector — hence
  [[Journal Entry Controls]] and close-automation tools
  (reconciliation and JE documentation) matter to SOX audits.
