Business combinations (acquisitions), accounted for under ASC 805, are among the most complex, judgment-intensive areas of financial reporting. [[FEI]]'s Committee on Corporate Reporting published a December 2019 preparer guide on designing and operating effective [[ICFR]] over them for [[SOX Section 404]](a) compliance.

Key facts:

- Acquired assets and assumed liabilities are measured at acquisition-date fair value; the [[Purchase Price Allocation]] is one of the most critical and complex steps.
- Risks vary acquisition by acquisition; controls must be tailored to each company's [[Risk Assessment]], materiality, and transaction facts, with example risks bracketed to assertions (completeness, existence, valuation, accuracy).
- The area relies heavily on [[Management Review Controls]] due to complexity, subjectivity, and estimation uncertainty; the "relevant competent leader" may sit outside accounting (FP&A, tax, M&A).
- Other control areas: manual [[Journal Entry Controls]] for the acquisition, consolidation of the target, disclosures, measurement period adjustments, accounting policy alignment, and integrating the target into the acquirer's [[Control Environment]].
- [[EY]]: 75% of companies cover business combinations with SOX controls, and 67% experienced M&A in the past three years — a business event that drives control deficiencies.
