The Three Lines Model, maintained by the [[IIA]] and updated in 2020, assigns risk and control responsibilities across three organizational "lines." [[Deloitte]] uses it as the backbone for designing a [[SOX Compliance Program]] operating model.

Typical SOX roles per Deloitte's mapping:

- **First line (management/control owners):** identify risks of material misstatement and IT risks; design, execute, and document controls; lead [[Walkthrough]]s; retain evidence; remediate; provide quarterly [[SOX Section 302]] sub-certifications; monitor outsourced providers and evaluate annual [[SOC 1 Report]]s.
- **Second line (SOX function):** financial-statement-level risk assessment (including [[Fraud Risk]] and IT systems); guide control design; evaluate deficiency severity; monitor remediation; report to the [[Audit Committee]]; run SOX training; maintain the [[GRC Platform]].
- **Third line ([[Internal Audit]]):** test controls; report deficiencies with root cause; coordinate reliance and testing with the [[External Auditor]]; report to the audit committee.
- Deloitte offers three operating-model options: SOX owned solely by the third line; assurance in the third line plus a second-line SOX function; or a second-line SOX function that "owns" SOX while internal audit moves to a strategic "Internal Audit 4.0" model.
- Rising risk profiles (transformation, M&A, new IT systems) push SOX work out of the third line so internal audit can pursue strategic audits; IT skills can be deployed in all three lines.
