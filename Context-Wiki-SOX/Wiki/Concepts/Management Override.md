title: Management Override
description: The inherent ability of senior management to circumvent internal controls, a key inherent limitation of ICFR and a mandatory fraud-risk focus area.

---

Management override is senior management's ability to circumvent
controls that otherwise appear to be operating effectively. It is
cited across the sources as a core inherent limitation of
[[Internal Control]] and a reason [[ICFR]] provides only
[[Reasonable Assurance]].

Key facts:
- [[AS 2201]] requires the auditor to address controls over
  management override — significant unusual transactions, journal
  entries, related parties, estimates, and incentive-mitigating
  controls — as part of [[Fraud Risk]] work.
- The [[SEC]]'s management guidance lists controls over management
  override among the [[Entity-Level Controls]] with a central role
  in the evaluation.
- COSO's monitoring guidance says the board (including the
  [[Audit Committee]]) should arrange objective monitoring of
  risks senior management cannot self-monitor — such as override —
  often via the [[Internal Audit]] function.
- Controls subject to management override are higher-risk and
  warrant more evaluation evidence.
