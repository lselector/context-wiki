AI in SOX compliance refers to applying artificial intelligence (including machine learning and generative AI) to [[SOX Compliance Program]] work. [[Protiviti]]'s 2024 poll of 1,500+ audit and finance professionals found high perceived potential but low current adoption.

Key facts:

- **57%** see high or moderate potential for AI to transform SOX compliance, yet **78%** are not currently using AI; most organizations remain in understand/explore/experiment phases.
- Promising use cases (Protiviti): risk assessments; process mapping and risk/control documentation; evidence gathering and data extraction; scope and completeness validation; control testing efficiency; fraud detection via machine learning on transaction data; compliance reporting; and generating walkthrough documentation (narratives, process flows, test scripts) from recorded meetings.
- Barriers: integrating new technologies with existing systems (46%) and insufficient skilled personnel (41%); 62% feel they lack the necessary technology skills, but fewer than 10% treat related training as a high priority.
- Vendor embedding: [[Oracle]] Fusion Risk Management ships AI agents — Certification Advisor (plain-language briefings for [[User Access Review]] decisions), ERP Access Assistant (preventive SoD analysis on role requests), and Assurance Advisor (100+ algorithms flagging toxic access combinations and at-risk control objectives).
- AI and automation form stage 4 of the Protiviti/[[AuditBoard]] technology maturity model.
