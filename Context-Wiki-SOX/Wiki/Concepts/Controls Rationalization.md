Controls rationalization is the periodic reassessment and streamlining of the controls portfolio — eliminating redundant or duplicate controls, aligning controls to current business risks, and reducing testing burden and cost in a [[SOX Compliance Program]].

Key facts:

- [[EY]]: **49%** of companies reassess/rationalize controls annually; stated reasons are increasing efficiencies/synergies (86%), aligning SOX with business risks (72%), and reducing costs (59%).
- [[KPMG]]'s Controls Portfolio Analysis (CPAM) supports rationalization efforts by quantifying the portfolio and the [[Total Cost of Control]] — reducing risks and costs while increasing reliance and value.
- [[PwC]]'s Level 2 maturity ("Process Efficiencies / Controls Insights") centers on control optimization: eliminating redundant/duplicate controls and extending control automation.
- [[Deloitte]] flags excessive testing caused by too many controls as a stale-program indicator addressed by [[SOX Modernization]].
- Related practice from [[KPMG]]'s 404 guidance: prioritize key/primary controls and pick the control point that best addresses each risk — redundant controls need not all be tested.
