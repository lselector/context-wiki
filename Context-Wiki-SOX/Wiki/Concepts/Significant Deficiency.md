A significant deficiency is the middle tier of [[ICFR]] deficiency
severity — above a mere control deficiency, below a
[[Material Weakness]].

Key facts:
- Definition (per AS 5 / [[AS 2201]], later adopted by the [[SEC]]):
  "a deficiency, or a combination of deficiencies, in ICFR that is
  less severe than a material weakness, yet important enough to
  merit attention by those responsible for oversight of the
  company's financial reporting."
- This replaced the AS 2-era test ("more than remote likelihood of
  a more-than-inconsequential misstatement").
- Must be communicated in writing to the [[Audit Committee]] and
  the [[External Auditor]] (per [[SOX Section 302]] processes) but
  is not disclosed in the annual report.
- Auditors are not required to search for deficiencies below the
  material-weakness threshold, but must report those identified.
- Multiple significant deficiencies do not automatically aggregate
  to a material weakness, but must be evaluated individually and in
  combination (same account, common assertion).
