IT General Controls (ITGCs) are controls over the IT environment — SEC Release 33-8810 lists the classic domains: **program development, program change, computer operations, and access to programs and data**. They support the proper and consistent operation of automated [[IT Application Controls]], key reports, and other critical IT functionality.

Key facts:

- ITGCs matter through the GAIT "reliance chain": they do not directly misstate financial statements, so an ITGC deficiency is escalated to a [[Significant Deficiency]] or [[Material Weakness]] only via the [[GAIT Methodology]]'s staged likelihood/impact analysis.
- Per SEC guidance and [[AS 2201]], only ITGCs necessary for the consistent operation of other identified controls need evaluation; IT risk is assessed within the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]], not as a separate evaluation. Automated controls are generally lower risk when relevant ITGCs are effective.
- [[EY]]: ITGCs produced **the most open deficiencies** at the end of the SOX cycle — more significant deficiencies and material weaknesses than any other aspect of SOX — and were the most time-consuming to remediate; IT general/application controls were rated the most challenging area (64%).
- [[Protiviti]]: 44% say IT access controls and security is the area with the most challenges or deficiencies — often [[Segregation of Duties]] conflicts and gaps in user access reviews and terminations.
- COSO notes the reliability of automated monitoring tools ([[Continuous Controls Monitoring]]) itself depends on ITGCs; ERP audit trails and role controls (NetSuite, Oracle, SAP) sit within the ITGC security and [[Change Management]] domains.
