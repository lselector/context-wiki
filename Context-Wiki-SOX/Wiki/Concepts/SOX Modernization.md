SOX modernization is [[Deloitte]]'s theme (2022 point of view: "It's time to refresh and rethink SOX") that in the two decades since the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]], technology and business environments advanced while many companies' [[SOX Compliance Program]] did not — layering on controls and spending the same or more without extracting value.

Key facts:

- **Three levers:** (1) operating model optimization — governance structure, monitoring aligned to the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]], accountability, resourcing; (2) program enhancements — challenge the risk assessment, transform testing, real-time status visualizations, harmonize compliance efforts; (3) technology and automation — test up to 100% of certain populations instead of sampling, automate control operation and eligible manual processes, implement a [[GRC Platform]].
- **Benefits:** enhanced quality, deeper root-cause insights, increased efficiency (precision testing over "checklist" control work), and reduced total cost of compliance.
- **Stale-program warning signs:** underutilized technology/data, unchallenged methodology, sample-based testing inadequate for data growth (risking a [[Material Weakness]]), excessive testing from too many controls, and rising costs amid [[SEC]]/[[PCAOB]] expectations.
