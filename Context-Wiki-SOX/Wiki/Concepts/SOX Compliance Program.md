A SOX compliance program is the standing organizational capability — governance, roles, methodology, and technology — through which a public company sustains its [[SOX Section 302]] certifications and [[SOX Section 404]] assessment of [[ICFR]] under the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]].

Key facts:

- [[Deloitte]] argues a purpose-built operating model across the [[Three Lines Model]] is crucial: governance sets the structure, resources align to knowledge and authority, and interdependent relationships strengthen the control environment.
- Cost and effort keep rising: [[EY]] found 63% say SOX effort has **increased** since initial implementation; [[Protiviti]]'s 2024 poll found 71% report scope expansion and 52% report internal cost increases over two years, driven partly by [[PCAOB]] inspection pressure on [[External Auditor]]s.
- Only 36% of programs have a formal steering committee of all key stakeholders (EY); 87%+ aim for a lean, effective control framework with heavy auditor reliance on management's work.
- Maturity assessments ([[KPMG]] SOX Health Check, [[ICFR Maturity Model]]) benchmark strategy, [[Total Cost of Control]], risk assessment, technology leverage, and auditor alignment.
- Modernization levers ([[SOX Modernization]]): operating model optimization, program enhancements, and technology/automation including a [[GRC Platform]] and [[Continuous Controls Monitoring]].
