Financial reporting risks are the risks of material misstatement
of the financial statements. Identifying and assessing them is the
first step of the [[Management Assessment Process]] under the
[[SEC]]'s interpretive guidance (Release 33-8810).

Key facts:
- Identified using knowledge of the business and how GAAP applies
  to it — "what could go wrong" — including inherent
  internal/external risk factors, risks in authorizing, processing,
  and recording transactions, [[Fraud Risk]], and the risk of
  [[Management Override]].
- Management then evaluates whether controls placed in operation
  adequately address those risks; only controls that adequately
  address them need be identified and documented — not every
  control in a process.
- Changes in risks (new systems, new standards, M&A) must be
  reassessed; the risks drive the evidence needed about control
  operation in the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]].
