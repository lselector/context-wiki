Monitoring Activities form the fifth component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]: [[Ongoing Monitoring]], [[Separate Evaluations]], or a combination ascertain whether each of the five components is present and functioning. COSO's January 2009 Guidance on Monitoring Internal Control Systems (authored by [[Grant Thornton]]) elaborates the component, helping organizations "build in" rather than "add on" monitoring in response to [[SOX Section 404]] cost pressures.

Key facts:

- Covers COSO principles 16-17: performing ongoing and/or separate evaluations, and communicating deficiencies timely to those responsible for corrective action.
- Monitoring provides value three ways: supports conclusions that all five components operate effectively (backing [[SOX Section 302]]/404 assertions); gives timely evidence of change so deficiencies are corrected early; and promotes good control operation through oversight.
- COSO's three-stage model: (1) establish a foundation ([[Tone at the Top]], organizational structure, competent and objective evaluators, a supported baseline); (2) design and execute procedures (prioritize risks, identify [[Key Controls]], identify [[Persuasive Information]], implement monitoring); (3) assess and report results, escalating severe deficiencies to the board.
- The board should arrange objective monitoring of controls senior management cannot self-monitor — notably [[Management Override]] risk — often via [[Internal Audit]].
- Well-tuned monitoring (objective evaluators, direct information, formal documentation) supports [[External Auditor]] reliance under [[AS 2201]].
- Analogous to the Review and Revision component of the [[COSO ERM Framework]].
