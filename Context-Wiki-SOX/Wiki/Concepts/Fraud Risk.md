title: Fraud Risk

Fraud risk is the risk that financial statements are materially
misstated due to intentional acts — financial statement
manipulation or asset misappropriation. Addressing it is mandatory
in both the [[Management Assessment Process]] and the
[[Integrated Audit]].

Key facts:
- AS 5 (now [[AS 2201]]) moved the fraud-risk discussion to the
  front of the standard; the auditor's fraud-risk assessment from
  the financial statement audit feeds the [[ICFR]] audit.
- Focus areas: controls over [[Management Override]], significant
  unusual transactions, [[Journal Entry Controls]], related-party
  transactions, management estimates, and controls that mitigate
  fraud incentives/pressures.
- COSO 2013 Principle 8 requires considering the potential for
  fraud when assessing risks; antifraud programs are among the
  controls management should test per the [[KPMG]] FAQ.
- Senior-management fraud of any magnitude is an indicator of a
  [[Material Weakness]].
- Hybrid/remote work and shared services increase fraud and
  [[Segregation of Duties]] exposure (Protiviti 2024).
