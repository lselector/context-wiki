title: Integrated Audit

An integrated audit is an audit of [[ICFR]] performed together
with the financial statement audit, designing control tests to
serve both objectives: the ICFR opinion as of year-end and
control-risk assessments for the financial statement audit.

Key facts:
- Required for accelerated filers by [[SOX Section 404]](b); the
  attestation is not a separate engagement (SEC Release 33-8238).
- Governed by [[AS 2201]] for issuers and [[AU-C 940]] for
  non-issuers engaged voluntarily or by mandate (e.g., FDICIA
  banks).
- The auditor uses the same control criteria (in practice the
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]) and the same materiality as
  management and the financial statement audit.
- Outputs: an opinion on ICFR effectiveness (adverse if any
  [[Material Weakness]] exists), written communication of
  deficiencies, and evaluation of management's report elements.
