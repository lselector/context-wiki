Separate evaluations are periodic assessments of [[Internal Control]] conducted outside routine operations — often by [[Internal Audit]] or other objective evaluators — in contrast to [[Ongoing Monitoring]] built into daily activities. Both are means of implementing the monitoring component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] (COSO principle 16: "Selects, develops, and performs ongoing and/or separate evaluations").

Key facts:

- Per COSO's monitoring guidance, separate evaluations are typically **more objective** and serve to reconfirm that ongoing monitoring is functioning as intended.
- **Higher risk shortens the interval** between separate evaluations of a given area.
- Evaluators need adequate **competence** (knowledge of the controls and what constitutes a deficiency) and **objectivity** (no vested interest in the results).
- COSO recommends ongoing monitoring over separate evaluations where feasible, with separate evaluations layered on for periodic reconfirmation.
- For outsourced controls, a user organization's own separate evaluations (or service auditor reports such as a [[SOC 1 Report]]) provide the needed monitoring of service providers.
