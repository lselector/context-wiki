Risk assessment is the second component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]: a dynamic, iterative process for identifying and analyzing risks to the achievement of objectives, forming the basis for how risks are managed.

Key facts:

- Covers COSO principles 6-9: specifying objectives clearly, identifying and analyzing risks across the entity, considering the potential for fraud (see [[Fraud Risk]]), and identifying changes that could significantly impact internal control.
- Under SEC Release 33-8810 and [[AS 2201]], risk assessment drives the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] approach: identify [[Financial Reporting Risks]], then the controls that adequately address them, with evidence scaled to risk.
- COSO monitoring guidance links monitoring design to risk prioritization — focusing on "meaningful risks" and [[Key Controls]].
- [[EY]] found 91% of companies use quantitative and qualitative data for scoping, but 46% scope only once a year; EY recommends refreshing scoping at least semiannually.
- [[Deloitte]] urges challenging the SOX risk assessment as a program enhancement lever; [[Protiviti]] reports thorough risk assessments among external auditors' top SOX focus areas (27%).
- Extended by the Performance component of the [[COSO ERM Framework]] (identify, assess severity, prioritize against [[Risk Appetite]]).
