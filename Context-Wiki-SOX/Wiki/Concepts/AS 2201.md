AS 2201, "An Audit of Internal Control Over Financial Reporting
That Is Integrated with An Audit of Financial Statements," is the
[[PCAOB]] standard for the [[Integrated Audit]] required by
[[SOX Section 404]](b). It was adopted as Auditing Standard No. 5
by Release 2007-005A (June 12, 2007), superseding AS 2, and later
recodified as AS 2201.

Key facts:
- Objective: opine on ICFR effectiveness; because [[ICFR]] cannot
  be effective if a [[Material Weakness]] exists, the auditor seeks
  reasonable assurance about whether material weaknesses exist as
  of the assessment date.
- Structured around a [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]]; the auditor
  must evaluate the [[Control Environment]] and the
  [[Period-End Financial Reporting Process]] and address
  [[Fraud Risk]] and [[Management Override]] controls.
- Uses the same framework and materiality as management; scales for
  smaller companies; permits using the work of others based on
  competence and objectivity; inquiry alone is never sufficient.
- Definitions of material weakness and [[Significant Deficiency]]
  are aligned with [[SEC]] rules (33-8809); one material weakness
  compels an adverse opinion.
- The [[AICPA]] non-issuer counterpart is [[AU-C 940]].
