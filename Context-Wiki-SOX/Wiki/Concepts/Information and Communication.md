Information and Communication is the fourth component of the [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]: relevant, quality information from internal and external sources supports the functioning of [[Internal Control]], and communication flows up, down, and across the entity and with external parties.

Key facts:

- Covers COSO principles 13-15: obtaining or generating and using relevant, quality information; internally communicating internal control information and responsibilities; and communicating with external parties regarding internal control matters.
- Quality of information underpins related SOX topics such as [[Information Produced by the Entity]] and [[Persuasive Information]] used in monitoring.
- The [[COSO ERM Framework]] has a parallel component — Information, Communication, and Reporting (principles 18-20: leverage information and technology; communicate risk information; report on risk, culture, and performance).
- COSO principle 17 (in Monitoring) requires deficiencies to be communicated timely to those responsible for corrective action, including senior management and the board.
