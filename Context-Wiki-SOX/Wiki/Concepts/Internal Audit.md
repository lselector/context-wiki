Internal audit is the organization's independent, objective
assurance function — the third line in the [[IIA]]
[[Three Lines Model]]. In SOX programs it commonly performs
control testing, reports deficiencies with root cause, and
coordinates with the [[External Auditor]] on reliance and samples.

Key facts:
- [[AS 2201]] lets the external auditor use internal auditors' work
  based on their competence and objectivity; as control risk
  increases the auditor must do more of the work personally.
- COSO monitoring guidance: the board uses internal audit for
  objective monitoring of controls senior management cannot
  self-monitor, such as [[Management Override]] risk.
- In Deloitte's operating model options, SOX may sit wholly within
  internal audit (Option 1) or move to a second-line SOX function,
  freeing internal audit for strategic audits ("Internal Audit
  4.0": assure, advise, anticipate, accelerate).
- Reports to the [[Audit Committee]]; internal audit activity is
  itself a monitoring [[Entity-Level Controls]] example.
