Entity-level controls (ELCs; the PCAOB's earlier term was
"company-level controls") are pervasive controls that operate
across the entity rather than at the transaction level. They play
a central role in the [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]].

Key facts:
- Examples per the [[SEC]]: the [[Control Environment]] (tone,
  integrity, board/audit committee oversight), controls over
  [[Management Override]], the risk assessment process,
  centralized/shared-service processing, monitoring controls
  ([[Internal Audit]], self-assessment), and
  [[Period-End Financial Reporting Process]] controls.
- AS 5 defines three precision categories: (1) indirect controls
  (control environment) affecting selection and testing of other
  controls; (2) monitoring controls that reduce (not eliminate)
  other testing; (3) controls precise enough to address a
  relevant-assertion risk on their own, eliminating other testing.
- Testing ELCs alone is not sufficient to conclude on [[ICFR]]
  effectiveness (KPMG FAQ), but a sufficiently precise ELC can
  eliminate the need to evaluate other controls for that risk.
- ELCs average only ~6% of in-scope key controls (EY survey), yet
  the auditor must evaluate the control environment and period-end
  process in every audit.
