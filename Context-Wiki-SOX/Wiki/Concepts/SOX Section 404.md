Section 404 of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] ("Management assessment
of internal controls," 15 U.S.C. 7262) is the heart of SOX
compliance work and the driver of most [[ICFR]] cost and effort.

Key facts:
- 404(a): each annual report must contain an internal control
  report stating management's responsibility for [[ICFR]] and
  management's assessment of its effectiveness as of fiscal
  year-end — the [[Management Assessment Process]].
- 404(b): the registered public accounting firm must attest to and
  report on ICFR (the [[Auditor Attestation Report]]) under
  [[PCAOB]] standards — today [[AS 2201]]; applies to accelerated
  filers; not a separate engagement. Section 405 exempts registered
  investment companies.
- Implemented by [[SEC]] Release 33-8238 (2003); made efficient by
  the 2007 package: management guidance (33-8810), rule amendments
  (33-8809), and AS 5.
- Management may not conclude ICFR is effective if one or more
  [[Material Weakness]]es exist; quarterly reports must disclose
  material changes in ICFR.
