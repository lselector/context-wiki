The management assessment process is the evaluation management
performs under [[SOX Section 404]](a) to state, in the annual
report, whether [[ICFR]] is effective as of fiscal year-end,
measured against a suitable framework such as the
[[COSO Internal Control Framework]].

Key facts:
- Two SEC organizing principles: (1) evaluate whether controls
  adequately address material-misstatement risk (a
  [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]]); (2) base evidence-gathering on
  risk — self-assessment and monitoring in low-risk areas, direct
  testing in high-risk areas.
- The SEC "daily interaction" concept: in smaller, centralized
  companies, management's daily interaction with its controls can
  itself provide evidence of operating effectiveness, limiting
  additional testing.
- Management may use a different approach than the auditor, need
  not identify every control, and must keep reasonable support
  (documentation) for the assessment; scope limitations are not
  permitted.
- KPMG's FAQ organizes the work into four phases: plan/scope,
  document and test design and operating effectiveness, correct
  deficiencies, and report; management's testing overall should be
  more comprehensive than the auditor's.
- Preconditions in [[AU-C 940]]: management acknowledges
  responsibility, uses suitable criteria, provides a written
  assessment, and gives the auditor full access.
