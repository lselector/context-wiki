A material weakness is the most severe category of [[ICFR]]
deficiency. The definition was codified by [[SEC]] Release 33-8809
(2007) and aligned with the [[PCAOB]]'s [[AS 2201]] so management
and auditors use one standard.

Key facts:
- Definition: "a deficiency, or a combination of deficiencies, in
  ICFR such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material
  misstatement of the registrant's annual or interim financial
  statements will not be prevented or detected on a timely basis."
  "Reasonable possibility" carries the FAS 5 meaning (reasonably
  possible or probable).
- If one exists, management may not conclude ICFR is effective, and
  the [[External Auditor]] must issue an adverse opinion; it must
  be disclosed in the annual report with nature, impact, and
  remediation plans.
- A material misstatement need not have occurred — severity depends
  on the possibility of misstatement.
- Indicators: senior-management fraud; restatement to correct a
  material misstatement; an auditor-identified material
  misstatement ICFR would not have detected; ineffective
  [[Audit Committee]] oversight.
- Severity is judged on likelihood and magnitude, individually and
  in aggregation, considering [[Compensating Controls]] and the
  "prudent official" test.
