Per Section 2 of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]], the audit committee
is a committee of the board of directors (or the whole board if
none exists) established to oversee the accounting, financial
reporting processes, and audits of the issuer.

Key facts:
- SOX Sec. 301: national exchanges may not list issuers without
  independent audit committees empowered to hire the
  [[External Auditor]] and handle whistleblower complaints;
  Sec. 407 requires disclosure of an audit committee financial
  expert.
- Receives written communication of all [[Material Weakness]]es
  and [[Significant Deficiency]] items before the audit report is
  issued; certifying officers disclose deficiencies and fraud to it
  under [[SOX Section 302]].
- Ineffective audit committee oversight is itself an indicator of a
  [[Material Weakness]] under [[AS 2201]].
- Board oversight of internal control is COSO 2013 Principle 2; the
  board also arranges objective monitoring of
  [[Management Override]] risk, often via [[Internal Audit]].
