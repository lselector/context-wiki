AU-C Section 940, "An Audit of Internal Control Over Financial
Reporting That Is Integrated With an Audit of Financial
Statements," is the [[AICPA]]'s counterpart to the [[PCAOB]]'s
[[AS 2201]] within [[GAAS]].

Key facts:
- Source: SAS No. 130 (amended by SAS Nos. 135, 140, 145, 146);
  effective for integrated audits for periods ending on or after
  December 15, 2016.
- Applies only when an auditor is **engaged** to audit [[ICFR]] —
  non-issuers are not subject to [[SOX Section 404]](b); typical
  cases are FDICIA banks, governmental or contractual mandates, and
  voluntary engagements.
- Preconditions: management acknowledges responsibility, evaluates
  ICFR against suitable criteria (e.g., the
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]), provides a written
  assessment, and supports it with documentation; refusal is a
  scope limitation.
- Parallels AS 2201: [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]], evaluation of
  whether the COSO components are present, functioning, and
  operating together, testing of [[Compensating Controls]] before
  reliance, adverse opinion for any [[Material Weakness]].
