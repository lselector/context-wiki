The external auditor is the independent registered public
accounting firm (registered with the [[PCAOB]]) that audits an
issuer's financial statements and, under [[SOX Section 404]](b),
attests to and reports on [[ICFR]] in an [[Integrated Audit]].

Key facts:
- Hired and overseen by the [[Audit Committee]] (SOX Sec. 301);
  Title II of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] bans specified non-audit
  services and requires audit partner rotation.
- Must gather independent evidence (inquiry, observation,
  inspection, [[Walkthrough]]s) — unlike management, which may rely
  on self-assessment and daily interaction.
- May use the work of internal auditors and others based on their
  competence and objectivity, scaled to control risk (AS 2201 /
  AU-C 610); EY found 40-43% reliance on management's testing.
- Independence limit: may assist in documenting controls only with
  active management involvement (Release 33-8238).
- Receives written communication of every [[Material Weakness]]
  and [[Significant Deficiency]]; issues the
  [[Auditor Attestation Report]].
