A SOC 1 report is a service auditor's report on a service organization's controls relevant to its customers' [[ICFR]]. Companies that outsource financially relevant processes or systems use SOC 1 reports to satisfy their obligation to monitor controls at service providers (COSO's monitoring guidance calls for independent audit/examination reports or the user's own [[Separate Evaluations]]).

Key facts:

- [[EY]]: **79%** of surveyed companies assess third-party service organizations via a SOC 1 report; 87% cover third-party service providers with SOX controls.
- In [[Deloitte]]'s [[Three Lines Model]] mapping, first-line control owners monitor outsourced service providers and evaluate annual SOC 1 reports.
- [[NetSuite]] undergoes SOC 1 Type II (and SOC 2 Type II) audits; auditors can rely on the cloud ERP vendor's SOC 1 report for the service organization's controls, reducing direct [[IT General Controls]] testing.
- SEC Release 33-8810 (referring to the predecessor Type 2 SAS 70 report): if a service organization refuses to provide one, management must decide whether that inability precludes concluding [[ICFR]] is effective — scope limitations are not permitted.
