Robotic Process Automation (RPA) uses software "bots" to automate repetitive, rules-based tasks. In the SOX context it appears twice: as a technology SOX teams can use to automate compliance work, and as a business technology whose bots themselves must be controlled and tested.

Key facts:

- [[EY]]: fewer than **16%** of SOX programs use RPA; where the business uses RPA, **66%** of SOX programs test the bots.
- [[Deloitte]]'s [[SOX Modernization]] lever includes automating eligible processes that are highly manual, frequent, and defined by standard tasks, so skilled resources can be redeployed.
- [[Protiviti]]/[[AuditBoard]]'s technology maturity model stage 4 ("AI and automation") envisions bots carrying out certain control procedures and document requests being eliminated.
- Candidate processes for automation per Deloitte: control testing (up to 100% of certain populations versus sampling) and control operation, increasing reliability.
