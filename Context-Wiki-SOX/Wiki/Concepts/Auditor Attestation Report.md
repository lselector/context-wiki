The auditor attestation report ("attestation report on internal
control over financial reporting") is the [[External Auditor]]'s
report on [[ICFR]] required by [[SOX Section 404]](b) and filed
with the annual report.

Key facts:
- SEC Release 33-8809 revised Regulation S-X Rule 2-02(f) to
  require a **single opinion directly on ICFR effectiveness**,
  eliminating the AS 2-era second opinion on management's
  assessment; the report was renamed accordingly.
- Required elements under [[AS 2201]]: opinion on whether the
  company maintained, in all material respects, effective ICFR
  based on the control criteria; basis for opinion; definition and
  inherent limitations of ICFR.
- One [[Material Weakness]] compels an adverse opinion; a scope
  limitation compels a disclaimer or withdrawal; refusal of written
  management representations is a scope limitation.
- The auditor also evaluates whether management's Item 308 report
  elements are complete and properly presented.
