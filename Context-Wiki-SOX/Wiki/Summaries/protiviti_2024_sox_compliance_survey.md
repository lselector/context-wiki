## Overview

This 2024 [[Protiviti]] report presents findings from an
interactive poll of more than **1,500 audit and finance
executives and professionals** attending a June 2024
webinar on SOX innovation hosted by Protiviti and
AuditBoard. More than two decades after the
[[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] was created to enhance corporate
governance and restore investor confidence, the report
finds SOX compliance has become more resource- and
time-intensive, and argues that analytics, automation, and
AI are the top opportunities to increase the efficiency and
effectiveness of the [[SOX Compliance Program]].

## Key takeaways

- The scope, hours, and costs of SOX compliance continue
  to increase, while budgets tighten.
- Most organizations are **not** currently using AI in
  their SOX programs, but acknowledge its transformative
  potential (**57%** see high or moderate potential;
  **78%** are not currently using AI).
- Only **one in three** organizations is maximizing
  enabling technologies to manage SOX compliance costs.
- Insufficient skilled personnel is a significant barrier —
  just **one in five** participants are highly confident
  they have the necessary skills.
- Yet fewer than **10%** treat training and development in
  analytics/automation as a high priority.

## Scope, hours and costs keep rising

- **32%** report SOX compliance scope significantly
  expanded and **39%** moderately increased over the past
  year; only 5% saw a decrease.
- **52%** report internal SOX compliance costs increased
  over the last two years (43% unchanged, 5% decreased).
- Drivers include the [[PCAOB]]'s increasing audit
  standards and enforcement actions, which pressure
  [[External Auditor]]s — and by extension management — to
  produce more documentation. PCAOB inspection reports
  cite audits lacking sufficient evidence for controls and
  insufficient procedures over key reports, information
  produced by the entity ([[Information Produced by the Entity|IPE]]), and
  [[Management Review Controls]].
- The [[SEC]]'s cybersecurity and climate disclosure rules
  add burden on the design of disclosure controls and
  procedures.
- External auditors are expanding audit scope and depth:
  their top SOX focus areas are detailed control
  documentation and evidence (31%), thorough risk
  assessments (27%), and accuracy/reliability of key
  reports (25%).
- Hybrid/remote work and shifts to shared services expose
  organizations to increased fraud risk and
  [[Segregation of Duties]] challenges.
- **44%** say IT access controls and security is the area
  with the most challenges or deficiencies — often stemming
  from [[Segregation of Duties]] conflicts and gaps in
  user access reviews and terminations, i.e. weaknesses in
  [[IT General Controls]]; ERP implementations and
  upgrades introduce further risk.
- Top approaches to manage costs: moving SOX activities
  in-house (28%), maximizing enabling technologies (35%),
  and utilizing lower-cost delivery options (41%).

## AI adoption: high potential, low usage

- Potential of AI to transform SOX compliance: 26% high,
  32% moderate, 29% some, 11% limited, 2% not a good
  match.
- Promising AI use cases: risk assessments; process
  mapping and risk/control documentation; evidence
  gathering and data extraction; scope and completeness
  validation; control testing efficiency; fraud detection
  via machine learning on transaction data; compliance
  reporting; and generating walkthrough documentation
  (narratives, process flows, test scripts) from recorded
  meetings.
- Most organizations remain in the understand/explore/
  experiment phases rather than adoption and integration.

## Analytics and automation: skills gap

- Per Protiviti's 2023 SOX Compliance Survey, only **38%**
  use data analytics and visualization platforms for SOX
  work; the 2024 poll shows three out of four use analytics
  tools minimally or in a limited manner (41% minimal/none,
  32% limited, 24% moderate, 3% extensive).
- Advanced users move toward
  [[Continuous Controls Monitoring]] and exception testing — e.g., analyzing 100%
  of journal entries and focusing on entries outside
  delegation of authority or booked on nonworking days;
  visualization tools identify [[Segregation of Duties]]
  violations at role and user level and support
  quantification of SoD deficiencies.
- Only ~13% are confident or very confident in using
  advanced analytics tools.
- Primary barriers: integrating new technologies with
  existing systems (46%) and insufficient skilled
  personnel (41%).
- Activities that would benefit most from automation:
  control testing (41%), monitoring and reporting (29%),
  risk assessment (17%), control documentation (12%).
- **62%** feel they lack the necessary technology skills;
  only 8% rate training in this area a high priority.

## Technology maturity model for SOX compliance

Protiviti (with AuditBoard) presents a four-stage maturity
model of increasing program efficiency:

1. **Manual** (MS Office, shared drives) — no real-time
   status, poor end-user experience.
2. **Purpose-built controls solution** ([[GRC Platform]] /
   audit management software) — automated dashboarding and
   reporting, improved control performance.
3. **Data analytics and automated controls** — declining
   deficiencies; manual daily controls transition to
   automated monthly controls.
4. **AI and automation** — document requests eliminated;
   bots carry out certain control procedures; optimized
   control environment.

Once a purpose-built solution is adopted, layering on
analytics, AI, and automation becomes significantly easier
and more effective.

## Call to action

- Make the most of existing tools (underutilized built-in
  platform capabilities).
- Develop a strategic technology road map aligned to
  business objectives and revisited periodically.
- Start small with pilot projects (e.g., automating
  document-gathering/evidence extraction) as proofs of
  concept.
- Challenge the internal team to develop critical skills
  in existing and emerging technologies.
- Leverage external expertise for road maps, use cases,
  and ready-to-go solutions.
- Embrace change and an innovation mindset; without a
  commitment to technology, SOX hours and resources will
  continue to rise.
