## Overview

This 19-page 2024 [[Deloitte]] advisory paper argues that
designing a purpose-built operating model is crucial to the
success of a [[SOX Compliance Program]]. The operating
model should match the mission and culture of the
organization and determine ownership and governance of the
program. Key benefits cited:

- **Governance setting the structure** — [[SOX Section 404]]
  requires an annual assessment by the CEO and CFO on the
  effectiveness of the system of [[Internal Control]]; an
  intentionally designed operating model provides the
  structures and processes to meet that objective.
- **Resource alignment and accountability** — assign
  resources based on knowledge, authority, and role.
- **Interdependent relationships** — end-to-end program
  responsibilities flow through strategically designed
  interdependencies that strengthen the control
  environment.

## SOX roles across the three lines

Deloitte maps typical SOX activities to the
[[Three Lines Model]] (per the IIA's 2020 update):

- **First line (management/control owners):** identify
  risks of material misstatement and IT risks; select and
  design controls; execute controls; maintain control
  documentation (narratives, flowcharts, risk control
  matrices); lead walkthroughs with internal and
  [[External Auditor]]s; retain audit evidence; develop
  remediation plans; certify through quarterly
  [[SOX Section 302]] sub-certifications; monitor
  outsourced service providers and evaluate annual
  [[SOC 1 Report]]s.
- **Second line (SOX function):** perform risk assessment
  at the financial statement level, including fraud risk,
  outsourced service providers, and IT systems; guide the
  first line on risk identification and control design;
  evaluate the severity of deficiencies; monitor and
  report deficiency remediation; report results to the
  [[Audit Committee]]; run the SOX training program;
  assess and report 302 certification results; implement
  and maintain the [[GRC Platform]].
- **Third line (internal audit):** receive the annual
  program scope from the second line; perform control
  testing; identify and report deficiencies with root
  cause; coordinate with the [[External Auditor]] on
  reliance, testing timelines, and samples; report to the
  [[Audit Committee]]; sit on the SOX Steering Committee.

Technology enablement (marked activities) is highlighted as
an optimization element across all three lines.

## Three operating model options

1. **Option 1 — SOX overseen solely by the third line:**
   the SOX function sits within [[Internal Audit]]
   (business process and IT internal audit) under the CAE,
   with first-line activities in the finance and IT
   organizations.
2. **Option 2 — Assurance in the third line plus a SOX
   function in the second line:** dedicated business
   process SOX and IT SOX teams under the CFO/CAO, with
   internal audit providing independent assurance.
3. **Option 3 — SOX function "owns" SOX in the second
   line:** a SOX Director under the CFO/CAO owns the
   program; internal audit shifts toward Deloitte's
   "Internal Audit 4.0" model (purpose-driven, digitally
   powered: assure, advise, anticipate, accelerate).

As an organization's risk profile increases (business
transformation, organic growth, emerging risks, new IT
systems, M&A), pressure grows to move SOX work out of the
third line so internal audit can pursue more strategic
operational and compliance audits.

## Deploying IT skills in the SOX program

- **IT in the first line:** own technical controls and
  execute them to mitigate IT risk.
- **IT in the second line:** design controls for
  technology-related risks; bring a technical lens to
  identify automation and efficiency opportunities;
  support scoping of key IT systems, automated controls,
  and key reports — strengthening [[IT General Controls]]
  and [[IT Application Controls]] coverage.
- **IT in the third line:** specialist testing of
  technology-risk controls and oversight of IT remediation
  plans.

Deloitte notes that expanding IT skills in the second line
can improve both efficiency and effectiveness and drive
continuous improvement.

## Case studies (second-line controllership structures)

1. **Global controller model** — governance and
   transformation, international markets, and domestic
   market workstreams; SOX scoping, certification
   management, analytics, and deficiency management under
   controllership, with [[IT General Controls]] handled by
   a separate technology team.
2. **CAO internal controls team (40+ FTEs)** — controls
   automation (including [[Continuous Controls Monitoring]]),
   financial integrity reviews of system implementations,
   and business process / IT general internal controls
   with [[GRC Platform]] ownership and quarterly control
   self-assessments.
3. **Split CAO/CIO model** — risk & controls directors
   under finance operations advise the first line, while
   an engineering compliance team under the CIO runs
   control automation, user access reviews, and maintains
   the in-scope SOX systems list.
4. **IT SOX under CTO/CIO** — a SOX Compliance Director
   and ITGC compliance managers provide oversight of the
   first line, liaise between internal and external audit,
   perform SOX readiness assessments, and coordinate
   deficiency remediation.
