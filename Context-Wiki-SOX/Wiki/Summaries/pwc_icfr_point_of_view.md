## Overview

This 2021 [[PwC]] Middle East paper positions [[ICFR]] as
an essential part of the CFO agenda: ensuring that reported
financial statement information is accurate and free of
material misstatement. It traces how [[Internal Control]]
and financial reporting disciplines evolved over two
decades — driven by the Enron collapse (which led to the
[[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]]), the 2008 global financial crisis,
oil price volatility, and Middle East corporate failures —
and presents PwC's maturity landscape and "FOCUSED"
methodology for extracting value from ICFR programs.

## Defining ICFR

PwC defines [[ICFR]] as a process implemented by those
charged with governance and management to provide
reasonable assurance that:

- Financial statements are prepared per the applicable
  reporting framework (e.g., IFRS, local GAAP).
- Transactions and events reported are duly authorized.
- A process exists to prevent or timely detect unauthorized
  use of assets.
- Accurate records/evidence back all reported transactions.

## Regulatory regime

International regulators driving the ICFR agenda include
the [[SEC]] and [[PCAOB]] (USA), the Council of the
European Union, the UK Financial Reporting Council, the
Canadian Securities Administrators, and India's Registrar
of Companies. Regional (Middle East) ICFR mandates cited:

- Qatar Financial Markets Authority (2016) — governance
  code for listed companies.
- UAE Securities and Commodities Authority (2016) —
  Resolution No. (7 R.M), Article 50.
- Abu Dhabi Accountability Authority (2017) — Resolution 1.
- UAE Insurance Authority (2019) — Circular no. 21.

## Stakeholder expectations and responsibilities

- **Regulators:** expect adoption of an internationally
  recognized framework (e.g., the
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]); responsible for
  monitoring implementation.
- **Investors, creditors, shareholders:** expect accurate
  financial reporting; encourage ICFR audit opinions and a
  strong control culture.
- **Statutory auditors ([[External Auditor]]s):** expect
  management to adopt an ICFR framework; independently
  assess management's ICFR practices.
- **Board / [[Audit Committee]]:** expect financial
  statements based on an effective ICFR framework; approve
  budgets, policies, and procedures.
- **Senior management (CEO/CFO):** sign off on the
  organization's conformance with effective ICFR practices
  (analogous to [[SOX Section 302]] certification).
- **Process owners:** comply with control practices in
  day-to-day operations.
- **Internal Control team (finance/risk):** lead the ICFR
  mandate; ensure controls are adequately designed and
  operating effectively.
- **[[Internal Audit]]:** advisory support for effective
  ICFR implementation.

## ICFR Maturity Landscape

PwC's maturity model has three levels assessed across four
dimensions (role clarity, governance culture, data
analytics, value):

1. **Level 1 — Regulatory Compliance:** ICFR seen as a
   regulatory burden; unclear roles; defensive culture
   (control failures hidden for fear of a qualified
   opinion); conventional sample testing; cost focus.
2. **Level 2 — Process Efficiencies / Controls Insights:**
   ICFR as an opportunity for process efficiency — control
   optimization, eliminating redundant/duplicate controls,
   extending control automation; responsive culture;
   intermediate analytics (full-population analysis for
   targeted sampling); efficiency focus.
3. **Level 3 — Value Enhancement:** formalized roles;
   collaborative culture with real-time correction of
   control failures; [[Continuous Controls Monitoring]]
   tools for critical controls; value focus on new
   projects and ventures.

## Survey statistics cited

- PwC 2020 Internal Controls survey: **76%** of respondents
  perceive [[Internal Control]] as valuable but feel not
  all organizational levels proactively participate; almost
  **60%** lack a specific governance, risk management and
  compliance system ([[GRC Platform]]) for managing
  internal control.
- PwC 2019 ICFR benchmarking survey: **41%** of companies
  have no scoping process to determine the extent of ICFR
  framework application; **60%** do not apply digital tools
  to support the ICFR process.

## PwC's FOCUSED approach

A seven-phase ICFR methodology built on leading practices
and frameworks such as [[COSO]] and SOX:

- **F**ramework development — entity-specific ICFR
  framework based on reporting standards and leading
  control practices.
- **O**perations assessment — identify the process
  universe, reporting risks, and mapping to financial
  statements.
- **C**ontrol design review — walkthroughs, data
  analytics, and control techniques to assess design
  adequacy.
- **U**pgrading internal practices — re-engineer processes
  and introduce digital practices to close design gaps.
- **S**ampling techniques — data-driven sampling for wider
  insight and assurance.
- **E**ffectiveness testing — mix of data analytics and
  conventional testing.
- **D**ocumentation and representation — governance, roles,
  timelines, and templates throughout the ICFR lifecycle.

## Case studies

- **Energy sector client:** data-analytics-driven
  re-analysis of the scoping model uncovered processes
  missed in prior ICFR exercises, fixing gaps that could
  have produced material errors.
- **Government-funded investment conglomerate (100+
  entities):** group-tailored ICFR framework plus a
  risk-factor data model to classify entities and steer
  effort toward the highest-risk controls — a form of
  [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] at group level.
