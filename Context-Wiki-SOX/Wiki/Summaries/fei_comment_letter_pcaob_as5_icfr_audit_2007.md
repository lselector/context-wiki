## Overview

On February 26, 2007, [[FEI]] (a leading organization of ~15,000 CFOs,
controllers, treasurers, and other senior financial executives) submitted
comments to the [[PCAOB]] on Rulemaking Docket 021 — the proposed
Auditing Standard No. 5, "An Audit of Internal Control Over Financial
Reporting That Is Integrated with An Audit of Financial Statements," and
the companion standard on using the work of others. AS 5 replaced the
costly, prescriptive AS 2 and was later recodified as [[AS 2201]]. The
letter transmits two responses: from FEI's Committee on Corporate
Reporting (CCR, chaired by Lawrence J. Salva) and its Small Public
Company Task Force. CCR strongly supports the shift to a
principles-based, judgment-driven [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] approach
to auditing [[ICFR]], seeing it as the key to restoring the cost/benefit
balance of [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] compliance.

## Two critical concerns

1. **Alignment with SEC management guidance.** The proposed standard,
   though much improved over AS 2, remained more detailed and
   prescriptive than the [[SEC]]'s proposed interpretive guidance for
   management (Release 33-8810). Misalignment would drive
   [[External Auditor]]s to a more conservative approach than
   management's assessment, forcing companies to keep documenting and
   testing lower-risk controls and incur unnecessary cost.
2. **PCAOB inspections must change too.** Unless [[PCAOB]] inspection
   criteria reward efficiency rather than only hunting for scope and
   procedure deficiencies, audit firms would take a wait-and-see approach
   for 12-18 months. FEI asked for concrete guidance on the expected
   extent of change, educational forums, inspection criteria supporting
   efficiency, and monitoring of firms' national-office guidance so it
   does not countermand the standard's intent.

## Top priority efficiency recommendations

- **Focus on change in controls** — allow rotational testing of controls
  that operated effectively in prior years and have not changed, and
  benchmarking beyond automated controls; limit walkthroughs to changes.
- **Increased reliance on company-level controls** — stronger language
  giving credit for effective [[Entity-Level Controls]] (both direct,
  e.g., analytic reviews and budget-to-actual comparisons, and indirect,
  e.g., ethics programs and board oversight) to reduce process-level
  testing; FEI also asked the PCAOB to align its "company-level controls"
  term with the SEC's "entity-level controls."
- **Remove "interim" financial statements** from the [[Material Weakness]] and [[Significant Deficiency]] definitions, so deficiencies
  are evaluated against annual materiality as of year-end, consistent
  with SEC guidance.
- **Reduce prescriptive provisions** — make walkthroughs a matter of
  auditor judgment; let the auditor verify that management evaluated all
  deficiencies rather than evaluating the severity of each deficiency
  himself; remove prescriptive competence/objectivity factors so
  auditors can rely on qualified line testers, not just
  [[Internal Audit]].

## Views on IT controls

FEI argued that [[IT General Controls]] had been extensively over-tested
even though an undetected material misstatement from them alone is
rarely reasonably possible; downstream [[Compensating Controls]]
(account reconciliations, supervisory reviews, external reporting
controls) usually mitigate the risk. It recommended focusing on the IT
control environment at the company level with rotational transaction-
level testing.

## Other positions

- **Deficiency definitions:** supported changing likelihood from "more
  than remote" to "reasonable possibility" and magnitude for significant
  deficiencies from "more than inconsequential" to "significant," and
  discouraged the mechanical big-firm deficiency evaluation framework.
- **Fraud:** clarify that the target is material misstatement due to
  fraud; treating senior-management fraud "of any magnitude" as a
  material weakness indicator should be tempered and the fraud types
  defined (financial statement manipulation vs. asset misappropriation).
- **Opinion on management's assessment:** supported eliminating the
  separate opinion on management's assessment process (retaining the
  opinion on controls under SOX Section 404(b)), and asked that the
  sample audit opinion wording be fixed accordingly.
- **Multi-location audits:** endorsed risk-based (rather than
  coverage-based) location scoping for decentralized companies.
- **Walkthroughs as evidence:** a walkthrough alone can suffice for
  lower-risk controls, entity-level controls, and smaller companies.
- **Effective date:** urged adoption early in calendar 2007, before
  design evaluations begin.

## Significance

The final AS 5 (adopted June 2007, now [[AS 2201]]) incorporated much of
what preparers like FEI advocated: a top-down, risk-based audit scoped by
[[Risk Assessment]], reliance on entity-level controls and the work of
others, and alignment with the SEC's management guidance — a milestone in
the post-2004 recalibration of [[SOX Section 404]] compliance economics.
