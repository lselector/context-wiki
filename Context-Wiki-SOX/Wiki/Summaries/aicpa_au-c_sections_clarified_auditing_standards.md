## Overview

This 2,046-page compilation, "U.S. Auditing Standards — AICPA
(Clarified) [AU-C]" (2025 edition), codifies the [[AICPA]] Auditing
Standards Board's Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) — i.e.,
Generally Accepted Auditing Standards ([[GAAS]]) for audits of
**non-issuers** (private companies and other entities outside [[PCAOB]]
jurisdiction). It spans a preface of principles, a glossary, and AU-C
sections organized by series: 200s (general principles and
responsibilities, e.g., AU-C 200 overall objectives, 230 documentation,
240 fraud), 300s–400s (risk assessment and response, e.g., AU-C 315),
500s (audit evidence), 600s (using the work of others, incl. AU-C 600
group audits and AU-C 610 internal auditors), 700s (reporting), 800s
(special considerations), and 900s (U.S. special considerations). For a
SOX-focused wiki the key section is **[[AU-C 940]]** (printed pages
1801–1878), the AICPA's counterpart to the PCAOB's [[AS 2201]].

## AU-C 940 — An Audit of ICFR Integrated with an Audit of Financial Statements

Source: SAS No. 130 (as amended by SAS Nos. 135, 140, 145, 146);
effective for integrated audits for periods ending on or after
December 15, 2016.

### Scope and objectives
- Applies only when an auditor is **engaged** to audit [[ICFR]]
  integrated with a financial statement audit (an [[Integrated Audit]])
  — typically banks/depository institutions (FDICIA), certain
  governmental or contractual mandates, or voluntary engagements, since
  non-issuers are not subject to [[SOX Section 404]](b).
- Objectives: obtain reasonable assurance about whether a
  [[Material Weakness]] exists as of the date of management's
  assessment, and express a written opinion on ICFR effectiveness,
  communicating with management and those charged with governance.

### Definitions and preconditions
- Defines [[ICFR]] essentially as the [[SEC]] rules do (records
  maintenance; recording per the applicable framework and authorized
  receipts/expenditures; prevention or timely detection of unauthorized
  asset acquisition, use, or disposition), noting inherent limitations —
  human failure, collusion, [[Management Override]].
- Preconditions: management must acknowledge responsibility for
  designing, implementing, and maintaining effective ICFR; evaluate ICFR
  using **suitable and available criteria** (e.g., the
  [[COSO Internal Control Framework]]); provide a written
  [[Management Assessment Process]] report accompanying the auditor's
  report; support the assessment with sufficient documentation; and give
  the auditor full access. Refusal to provide a written assessment is a
  scope limitation.
- The auditor uses the **same criteria** and the **same materiality** as
  management/the financial statement audit.

### Audit approach (parallel to AS 2201)
- Integrate testing to support both the ICFR opinion and control risk
  assessments; focus on higher-risk areas and [[Fraud Risk]], including
  management override.
- Use a [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]]: identify and test
  [[Entity-Level Controls]]; evaluate whether the components of ICFR
  are **present, functioning, and operating together** (COSO language);
  evaluate the [[Period-End Financial Reporting Process]]; identify
  significant classes of transactions, account balances, disclosures,
  and relevant assertions via inherent risk factors (SAS 145);
  understand transaction flows, IT effects, and general IT controls
  ([[IT General Controls]]); select and test controls that address the
  assessed risks.
- Test design effectiveness and operating effectiveness; scale evidence
  to control risk; use rollforward procedures from interim testing; vary
  testing year to year for unpredictability. The
  [[External Auditor]] may use the work of internal auditors or others
  (AU-C 610) scaled to competence, objectivity, and control risk.

### Deficiencies, opinions, and communications
- Evaluate the severity of each deficiency, individually and in
  combination, to identify any [[Material Weakness]] or
  [[Significant Deficiency]]; test [[Compensating Controls]] before
  relying on them; apply the prudent-official test.
- A material weakness requires an **adverse opinion**; inability to
  obtain evidence requires disclaiming; subsequent-events procedures
  cover the period up to the report date.
- The auditor evaluates management's report elements (responsibility
  statement, subject matter, criteria, assessment, material weaknesses,
  as-of date), obtains written representations, and communicates
  material weaknesses and significant deficiencies in writing to
  management and those charged with governance. Exhibits provide
  illustrative auditor reports, deficiency communications, and a
  management report.

## Compilation context

- The AU-C sections resulted from the AICPA Clarity Project, aligning
  GAAS with International Standards on Auditing while retaining U.S.
  requirements; each section follows a uniform structure (Introduction,
  Objectives, Definitions, Requirements, Application material).
- Other sections relevant to SOX-adjacent work: AU-C 240 (fraud),
  AU-C 265 (communicating internal control matters), AU-C 315 (risk
  assessment), AU-C 402 (service organizations), AU-C 610 (internal
  auditors), AU-C 930 (interim financial information), AU-C 935
  (compliance audits).
