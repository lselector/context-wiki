## Overview

This 2022 [[Deloitte]] point of view (a one-page brief)
argues that in the two decades since the
[[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] of 2002 was enacted, technology,
methodology, and business environments have advanced
significantly, but the [[SOX Compliance Program]] at many
companies has not evolved at the same pace. Instead,
companies have layered on additional controls while
spending the same or more to achieve compliance, without
extracting value. Deloitte's message: "It's time to refresh
and rethink SOX" — the core theme of [[SOX Modernization]].

## Levers of SOX transformation

Deloitte identifies three levers for modernizing a
[[SOX Compliance Program]]:

### 1. Operating model optimization

- Define the governance structure.
- Establish a monitoring program aligned with the
  [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] (risk assessment).
- Drive accountability by defining roles and
  responsibilities.
- Determine what resources are needed and when.

### 2. Program enhancements

- Challenge the SOX risk assessment to refresh the
  risk-based approach.
- Transform testing strategies.
- Enable real-time status updates with visualizations.
- Harmonize risk assessment efforts across other
  compliance activities throughout the organization.

### 3. Technology and automation

- Automate control testing to test up to 100% of certain
  populations (versus traditional sampling).
- Automate control operation to increase reliability.
- Automate eligible processes that are highly manual,
  frequent, and defined by standard tasks, so resources
  can be redeployed.
- Implement a [[GRC Platform]] (governance, risk, and
  control tool).

## Benefits of SOX modernization

- **Enhanced quality** — increased transparency and
  visibility into business processes, with meaningful
  insights for managing risks.
- **Deeper insights** — move away from point-in-time
  solutions toward addressing issues at their root cause.
- **Increased efficiency** — sharper focus on risks and
  key controls using precision testing methods; move away
  from the "checklist" approach to [[Internal Control]].
- **Reduced total cost of compliance** — create
  shareholder value by redeploying highly skilled
  resources to revenue-driving or operational work.

## Indicators of a stale SOX program

Deloitte lists warning signs in three categories:

### Hinders performance

- Technology, automation, and data are underutilized.
- Methodology is not challenged or developed.
- Resources focus on executing just-in-time rather than
  strategic activities.

### Insufficient assurance

- Ineffective use of a risk-based approach may expose the
  company to a [[Material Weakness]].
- Sample-based testing may be inadequate given the
  exponential growth in data volumes.

### High cost of compliance

- Excessive testing caused by too many controls and
  inefficient testing approaches.
- Untimely results despite high spend.
- Increasing forecasted costs given the regulatory
  environment (e.g., [[SEC]] and [[PCAOB]] expectations).
- Lack of coordination across compliance functions.
