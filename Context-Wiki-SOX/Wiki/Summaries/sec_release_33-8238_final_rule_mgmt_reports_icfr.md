## Overview

SEC Release Nos. 33-8238 / 34-47986 (June 5, 2003) is the foundational
implementing rule for [[SOX Section 404]] of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]].
It requires every Exchange Act reporting company (other than registered
investment companies) to include in its annual report a report of
management on the company's [[ICFR]] plus the [[External Auditor]]'s
attestation report. It also amends the [[SOX Section 302]] certification
rules and requires Section 302 and [[SOX Section 906]] certifications to
be filed as exhibits to annual and quarterly reports so investors, the
SEC, and the Department of Justice can monitor compliance via EDGAR.

## Definition of internal control over financial reporting

The rule adopted the now-standard term "internal control over financial
reporting" and defined it as a process designed by or under the
supervision of the principal executive and financial officers, effected
by the board, management, and other personnel, to provide reasonable
assurance regarding the reliability of financial reporting and
preparation of GAAP financial statements, including policies and
procedures that:

1. Pertain to maintaining records that accurately and fairly reflect
   transactions and dispositions of assets;
2. Provide reasonable assurance that transactions are recorded as
   necessary for GAAP financial statements and that receipts and
   expenditures occur only per management/director authorization; and
3. Provide reasonable assurance regarding prevention or timely detection
   of unauthorized acquisition, use, or disposition of assets
   ([[Safeguarding of Assets]]) that could materially affect the
   financial statements.

The release traces the lineage of [[Internal Control]] from the
[[Foreign Corrupt Practices Act]] of 1977 through the Treadway
Commission to [[COSO]]'s 1992 Internal Control — Integrated Framework
and its 1994 safeguarding-of-assets addendum, and deliberately limits
the definition to the financial-reporting subset of the
[[COSO Internal Control Framework]] (not operations or broad compliance
objectives).

## Required contents of management's annual internal control report

The internal control report must contain:

- A statement of management's responsibility for establishing and
  maintaining adequate [[ICFR]];
- A statement identifying the evaluation framework used by management;
- Management's assessment of ICFR effectiveness as of fiscal year-end,
  stating whether ICFR is effective and disclosing any
  [[Material Weakness]] identified — management may **not** conclude
  ICFR is effective if one or more material weaknesses exist; and
- A statement that the registered public accounting firm has issued an
  attestation report on management's assessment (the attestation report
  must be filed with the annual report and cannot be a separate
  engagement, per [[PCAOB]] attestation standards).

## Evaluation framework and process

- Management must base its evaluation on a **suitable, recognized
  control framework** established by a due-process body — free from
  bias, permitting consistent measurement, sufficiently complete, and
  relevant to ICFR. The [[COSO Internal Control Framework]] qualifies
  but is not mandated (CoCo and the Turnbull Report are alternatives).
- Auditor independence: the [[External Auditor]] may assist in
  documenting controls only with active management involvement — a
  reminder tied to the SEC's non-audit-services independence rules and
  the [[Management Assessment Process]].

## Quarterly and certification requirements

- **Quarterly evaluations:** rather than a full quarterly ICFR
  evaluation, companies must disclose any change in ICFR during the
  fiscal quarter that has materially affected, or is reasonably likely
  to materially affect, ICFR.
- The release distinguishes ICFR from the broader
  [[Disclosure Controls and Procedures]] evaluated under
  [[SOX Section 302]], and revised the certification forms accordingly.
- [[SOX Section 906]] certifications (18 U.S.C. 1350, with criminal
  penalties) must accompany periodic reports as exhibits.

## Scope and transition

- Applies to domestic issuers, foreign private issuers (Forms 20-F,
  40-F), small business issuers, and bank/thrift holding companies;
  registered investment companies are exempt.
- The release established phased compliance dates (later extended in
  subsequent releases) beginning with accelerated filers in 2004.
