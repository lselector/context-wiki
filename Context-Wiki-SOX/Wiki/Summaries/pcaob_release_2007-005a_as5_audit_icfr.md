## Overview

PCAOB Release No. 2007-005A (June 12, 2007) adopts Auditing Standard
No. 5, "An Audit of Internal Control Over Financial Reporting That Is
Integrated with An Audit of Financial Statements" — since recodified as
[[AS 2201]] — together with an independence rule (Rule 3525 on
internal-control non-audit services) and conforming amendments. AS 5
superseded Auditing Standard No. 2, the [[PCAOB]]'s original standard
under Sections 103 and 404(b) of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]]. The Board's
monitoring showed the [[ICFR]] audit produced real benefits (better
governance, higher-quality reporting) but at greater-than-expected cost;
AS 5 was designed to focus auditors on what matters most, eliminate
unnecessary procedures, improve scalability for smaller companies, and
raise the likelihood that a [[Material Weakness]] is found before it
causes material misstatement. The release drew 175 comment letters and
was closely coordinated with the [[SEC]]'s management guidance
(Release 33-8810), which the SEC approved in parallel.

## Notable areas of change from AS 2 / the proposal

### Alignment with SEC management guidance
- AS 5 uses the same definitions of [[Material Weakness]] and
  [[Significant Deficiency]], the same material-weakness indicators, and
  the same term "[[Entity-Level Controls]]" as the SEC's final guidance.
- The release stresses the different roles: management's daily
  involvement lets it rely on self-assessment and monitoring; the
  independent auditor must gather evidence through inquiry, observation,
  inspection, and walkthroughs to support an opinion.

### The top-down approach
- The standard is structured around a [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]]:
  financial-statement level risks → entity-level controls → significant
  accounts and disclosures and relevant assertions → controls to test.
- Requirements to separately identify "major classes of transactions"
  and "significant processes" were dropped to avoid a checklist
  mentality; the end point remains testing controls that address the
  assessed risk of misstatement for each relevant assertion.

### Emphasis on fraud controls
- Fraud-risk discussion moved to the front of the standard; the
  auditor's [[Fraud Risk]] assessment from the financial statement audit
  feeds the ICFR audit, with focus on controls over
  [[Management Override]], significant unusual transactions, journal
  entries, related-party transactions, and management estimates.

### Entity-level controls
- Three categories: (1) indirect controls (control environment) that
  affect selection and testing of other controls; (2) monitoring
  controls that can reduce (but not eliminate) testing of other
  controls; (3) controls precise enough to sufficiently address a
  relevant-assertion risk by themselves, eliminating other testing.

### Walkthroughs
- The rigid AS 2 walkthrough requirement was replaced by
  objectives-based requirements for understanding likely sources of
  misstatement; a [[Walkthrough]] is usually the most effective way to
  meet them but is not mandated mechanically.

### Deficiency evaluation and communication
- [[Significant Deficiency]] redefined as "a deficiency, or a
  combination of deficiencies, in ICFR that is less severe than a
  material weakness, yet important enough to merit attention by those
  responsible for oversight of the company's financial reporting."
- Auditors are not required to search for deficiencies below the
  material-weakness threshold but must communicate identified
  significant deficiencies to the [[Audit Committee]] in writing.
- Material weakness definition minor-tuned to match the SEC's rule
  ("reasonable possibility ... annual or interim ... prevented or
  detected on a timely basis").

### Scalability and using the work of others
- Explicit direction on scaling the audit for smaller, less complex
  companies (one of the Board's four-point plan goals).
- The separately proposed standard "Considering and Using the Work of
  Others" was **not** adopted; instead AS 5 incorporates principles
  allowing the [[External Auditor]] to use the work of internal auditors
  and others based on their competence and objectivity and on the risk
  of the control — supporting reliance on
  [[Internal Audit|Internal Audit Function]] testing.
