## Overview

SEC Release Nos. 33-8809 / 34-55928 (final rule, effective August 27,
2007) is the companion rulemaking to interpretive Release 33-8810. It
amends Exchange Act Rules 13a-15(c), 15d-15(c), and 12b-2, Rules 1-02
and 2-02 of Regulation S-X, and Item 308 of Regulations S-B/S-K to make
[[SOX Section 404]] evaluations by management and audits by the
[[External Auditor]] more effective and efficient. It was coordinated
with the [[PCAOB]]'s adoption of Auditing Standard No. 5 (now
[[AS 2201]]) so that management guidance and auditing standards use the
same key definitions and concepts.

## Amendment 1 — Evaluation safe harbor (Rules 13a-15(c) / 15d-15(c))

- The amended rules state that an [[ICFR]] evaluation conducted in
  accordance with the Commission's interpretive guidance (Release
  33-8810) **is one way** to satisfy the annual evaluation requirement.
- Compliance with the guidance is voluntary; there are many acceptable
  ways to evaluate ICFR, and companies with existing
  [[Management Assessment Process]] approaches need not change them.
- The SEC declined to codify prescriptive criteria, preferring the
  principles-based [[Top-Down Risk Assessment]] approach while leaving
  room for alternatives (e.g., approaches integrated with enterprise
  risk management).

## Amendment 2 — Auditor's attestation report (Regulation S-X)

- Rule 2-02(f) was revised to require the auditor to express a **single
  opinion directly on the effectiveness of ICFR**, eliminating the
  separate opinion on management's assessment that Auditing Standard
  No. 2 had required.
- The report is renamed an "attestation report on internal control over
  financial reporting" (not "on management's assessment of ICFR").
- The SEC reasoned that an audit restricted to evaluating what
  management did would not give sufficient assurance for an independent
  opinion, and that a direct opinion is consistent with both Section
  404(b) and Section 103(a)(2)(A)(iii) of the [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]]
  (audit-report content: findings, evaluation of the control structure,
  and description of material weaknesses).
- The auditor still evaluates whether management's Item 308 disclosures
  are complete and fairly stated, and modifies the report if not.
- Transition: until AS 5 replaced AS 2, companies could file either the
  two-opinion (AS 2) or single-opinion report.

## Amendment 3 — Definition of Material Weakness (Rule 12b-2 / Rule 1-02)

- The rules now define a [[Material Weakness]] as "a deficiency, or a
  combination of deficiencies, in ICFR such that there is a **reasonable
  possibility** that a material misstatement of the registrant's annual
  or interim financial statements will not be prevented or detected on a
  timely basis."
- "Reasonable possibility" carries the FAS 5 meaning (reasonably
  possible or probable); the SEC rejected alternatives such as
  "reasonable likelihood" or a more-than-50% threshold, judging they
  would weaken investor protection without meaningful efficiency gains.
- The reference to **interim** financial statements was retained:
  deficiencies posing a reasonable possibility of material misstatement
  in quarterly reports must be disclosed and preclude an effective-ICFR
  assessment.
- The definition was aligned with the PCAOB's [[AS 2201]] definition so
  management and auditors use one standard.
- A definition of [[Significant Deficiency]] was deferred to a separate
  release seeking additional comment (later adopted as a deficiency less
  severe than a material weakness yet important enough to merit
  attention by those responsible for oversight of financial reporting).

## Context and rationale

- Responds to widespread feedback (200+ comment letters) that initial
  [[SOX Section 404]] implementation costs — particularly under AS 2 —
  exceeded expectations, especially for smaller companies.
- Part of a coordinated SEC/PCAOB package: interpretive guidance for
  management (33-8810), rule amendments (33-8809), a request for comment
  on "significant deficiency" (34-55930), and PCAOB adoption of AS 5
  (Release 2007-005A).
