## Overview

The [[Sarbanes-Oxley Act]] of 2002 (Public Law 107-204,
H.R. 3763, enacted July 30, 2002) is a U.S. federal law
"to protect investors by improving the accuracy and
reliability of corporate disclosures made pursuant to the
securities laws." It responds to major accounting scandals
by creating the [[PCAOB]], strengthening
[[External Auditor]] independence, and making CEOs and
CFOs personally accountable for financial reports.
Enforcement authority rests with the [[SEC]]; violations
are treated like violations of the Securities Exchange Act
of 1934.

## Structure: the eleven titles

- Title I — Public Company Accounting Oversight Board:
  establishes the [[PCAOB]] (Secs. 101-109) to register,
  inspect, investigate, and discipline registered public
  accounting firms and to set auditing standards.
- Title II — Auditor Independence: bans specified
  non-audit services, requires [[Audit Committee]]
  preapproval of services, audit partner rotation, and
  auditor reports to audit committees (Secs. 201-209).
- Title III — Corporate Responsibility: audit committee
  independence (Sec. 301), officer certification
  ([[SOX Section 302]]), prohibitions on improper
  influence on audits (Sec. 303), forfeiture of
  bonuses/profits after restatements ("clawback",
  Sec. 304), and pension blackout trading rules.
- Title IV — Enhanced Financial Disclosures: off-balance
  sheet disclosures (Sec. 401), management assessment of
  internal controls ([[SOX Section 404]]), code of ethics
  (Sec. 406), audit committee financial expert (Sec. 407),
  real-time disclosure (Sec. 409).
- Title V — Analyst Conflicts of Interest.
- Title VI — Commission Resources and Authority.
- Title VII — Studies and Reports (GAO/SEC studies).
- Title VIII — Corporate and Criminal Fraud
  Accountability: document destruction penalties
  (Sec. 802), whistleblower protection (Sec. 806),
  securities-fraud criminal penalties (Sec. 807).
- Title IX — White-Collar Crime Penalty Enhancements,
  including CEO/CFO certification with criminal penalties
  ([[SOX Section 906]]).
- Title X — Sense of the Senate on CEO signing of
  corporate tax returns.
- Title XI — Corporate Fraud and Accountability
  (record tampering, SEC freeze authority, officer bars).

## Key definitions (Section 2)

- Issuer: a company with securities registered under the
  Exchange Act or required to file reports with the
  [[SEC]].
- [[Audit Committee]]: a board committee (or the whole
  board if none exists) overseeing accounting, financial
  reporting, and audits of the issuer.
- Registered Public Accounting Firm: a public accounting
  firm registered with the [[PCAOB]].
- Non-Audit Services: professional services other than
  audit/review of financial statements.

## Section 302 — Corporate responsibility for financial reports

[[SOX Section 302]] (15 U.S.C. 7241) requires the
principal executive and financial officers to certify in
each annual or quarterly report that:

- They reviewed the report; it contains no untrue material
  statement or material omission; and the financial
  statements fairly present the financial condition and
  results of operations.
- They are responsible for establishing and maintaining
  [[Internal Control]], have designed controls so material
  information is made known to them, have evaluated control
  effectiveness within 90 days of the report, and have
  presented their conclusions.
- They disclosed to the [[External Auditor]] and the
  [[Audit Committee]] all [[Significant Deficiency]]
  items and any [[Material Weakness]] in internal
  controls, plus any fraud (material or not) involving
  management or employees with a significant control role.
- They indicated any significant post-evaluation changes
  in internal controls, including corrective actions.

Foreign reincorporation does not lessen the force of the
certification.

## Section 404 — Management assessment of internal controls

[[SOX Section 404]] (15 U.S.C. 7262) has two parts:

- 404(a): the [[SEC]] must prescribe rules requiring each
  annual report to contain an internal control report that
  (1) states management's responsibility for establishing
  and maintaining an adequate internal control structure
  and procedures for financial reporting ([[ICFR]]), and
  (2) contains management's assessment, as of fiscal
  year-end, of the effectiveness of that structure — the
  basis of the [[Management Assessment Process]].
- 404(b): the registered public accounting firm auditing
  the issuer shall attest to, and report on, management's
  assessment, under [[PCAOB]] attestation standards (today
  [[AS 2201]]); the attestation is not a separate
  engagement.
- Section 405 exempts registered investment companies.

## Section 906 — Criminal certification

[[SOX Section 906]] adds 18 U.S.C. 1350: each periodic
report with financial statements must be accompanied by a
written CEO/CFO statement certifying that the report fully
complies with Exchange Act Sections 13(a)/15(d) and fairly
presents, in all material respects, the financial condition
and results of operations. Penalties: knowing violation —
up to $1,000,000 fine and/or 10 years in prison; willful
violation — up to $5,000,000 and/or 20 years.

## Other notable provisions

- Sec. 301: national exchanges may not list issuers
  without independent audit committees empowered to hire
  the [[External Auditor]] and handle whistleblower
  complaints.
- Sec. 304: CEO/CFO reimbursement of bonuses and stock
  profits after restatements caused by misconduct.
- Sec. 802: criminal penalties (up to 20 years) for
  altering or destroying records to obstruct
  investigations.
- Sec. 806: civil whistleblower protection for employees
  of publicly traded companies who report fraud.
- Sec. 108: recognition of accounting standards set by a
  qualifying standard-setting body.
